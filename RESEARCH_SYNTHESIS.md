## The Audience (5 Personas)

### 1. The Newcomer (small biz owner exploring GovCon)
- **Size:** Largest group, lowest willingness to pay ($20-50/mo)
- **Pain:** Overwhelming complexity, jargon barrier, "registered on SAM.gov 6 months ago and nothing happened"
- **Speaks:** "How do I get started?" / "Is 8(a) worth it?"
- **Needs:** Step-by-step guidance, jargon translation, curated opportunities

### 2. The Small Contractor ($2M-$25M) — PRIMARY TARGET
- **Size:** ~15K-20K companies, highest pain + budget to solve it ($100-300/mo)
- **Pain:** Finding opportunities before incumbents shape them, $10-50K proposal costs with 10-20% win rates, "is this wired?"
- **Speaks:** "Pipeline," "capture," "left of the RFP," "who's the incumbent?"
- **Needs:** Pre-RFP intelligence, competitor intel, recompete alerts, bid/no-bid guidance

### 3. The BD Professional (capture manager at mid-large firm)
- **Size:** ~10K-20K individuals, employer pays ($200-500/mo)
- **Pain:** Has GovWin but spends hours connecting dots. Needs synthesis, not more data.
- **Speaks:** "Capture plan," "gate review," "color team," "price-to-win," "shaping"
- **Needs:** Weekly synthesis brief, competitive intelligence, policy change impact analysis

### 4. The GovCon Advisor (consultant, attorney, PTAC counselor)
- **Size:** ~20K, moderate WTP ($100-200/mo)
- **Pain:** Keeping up with regulatory changes fast enough to advise clients
- **Needs:** White-label intelligence, regulatory summaries, client-ready content

### 5. The DOGE-Era Anxious Contractor
- **Size:** Huge (temporary), high urgency
- **Pain:** "Will my contract get cut?" No early warning system.
- **Needs:** Real-time DOGE tracking, agency-level impact analysis, pivot guidance

### Language to Use in Marketing
- "Capture" not "sales"
- "Pipeline" not "deal flow"
- "Left of the RFP" = before the solicitation drops
- "Wired" = pre-determined for incumbent
- "Bid/no-bid" = the decision whether to pursue
- "Set-aside" = reserved for small business categories
- "Recompete" = expiring contract that will be re-solicited
- "Bridge contract" = short extension while recompete is pending

## Newsletter Design: What the Research Says

### Current Template Assessment
The existing design is strong (table-based, inline CSS, navy/gold palette, color-coded sections). Refinements, not a rebuild.

### Must-Do Improvements
1. **Add Table of Contents** — readers lose their place in long emails. Morning Brew and CB Insights both do this.
2. **Add section numbering** (01 / Recompete Alerts) — wayfinding system like McKinsey/Gartner briefs
3. **Dark mode support** — meta tags + color-scheme CSS
4. **Outlook VML fallbacks** — bulletproof CTA button, ghost tables
5. **Mobile padding** — reduce 32px to 16px on small screens
6. **Preheader spacing hack** — prevent inbox from pulling body content
7. **Accessibility** — role="article", aria-roledescription="email", lang attributes

### Content Strategy Insights
- **Gate the analysis, not the data** — free tier sees recompete cards, paid tier gets "Intel:" callouts. "The data is the free sample, the insight is what you pay for."
- **3-tier visual hierarchy**: Scan layer (badges, numbers) > Read layer (titles, metadata) > Deep layer (analysis paragraphs)
- **Subject lines that work**: Specific number + urgency ("$890M in recompetes hitting in 60 days") NOT generic dates ("GovCon Weekly: March 16-22")
- **Send Monday 7 AM EST** — BD managers plan their week Monday morning
- **No images** — GovCon/defense audience behind VPNs, images fail to load. Text + structure is the right choice.
- **No emoji in subject lines** — this audience expects formality

### The Design Formula
```
AUTHORITY (navy + gold + dense data)
+ URGENCY (red badges + countdown days + action items)
+ STRUCTURE (numbered sections + TOC + consistent card pattern)
+ SCANNABILITY (3-tier hierarchy: skim > read > deep)
= The email BD managers forward to their CEO with "we need to subscribe to this"
```
