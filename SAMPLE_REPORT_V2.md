## Vertical Deep Dive: Cybersecurity

*This week's rotation. Next week: AI/ML.*

### The Numbers

Cybersecurity-related obligations totaled **$2.1B this quarter**, up 34% from Q1. This is not a blip — it's a structural shift with three compounding drivers:

1. **Zero trust deadlines are real.** OMB M-22-09 enforcement is creating non-discretionary spend. Agencies that delayed are now panic-buying.
2. **CMMC 2.0 is driving downstream demand.** Every defense contractor needs assessment and remediation services. The DIB is a second customer base hiding inside DoD budgets.
3. **Post-breach investment cycle continues.** SolarWinds and MOVEit accelerated security budgets that haven't slowed down.

### Trend: Zero Trust Is Now a Budget Line Item

Zero trust went from buzzword to procurement category in under 18 months. DISA alone has $937M in active cyber contracts hitting recompete or expansion this quarter. The agencies that were "evaluating zero trust architectures" in FY24 are now issuing production contracts.

### So What: What You Should Do

- **If you're a cyber firm under $50M revenue:** Don't chase the EITSS-III or NOCDef primes directly. Your play is CMMC assessment services for the defense industrial base — same budget pool, less competition, faster sales cycle.
- **If you're a mid-tier integrator:** The NOCDef restructuring into multiple task areas is your opening. CYBERCOM is explicitly signaling they want to break the single-vendor dependency. Start identifying which of the 3 task areas aligns with your past performance.
- **If you're a large prime:** The DES ceiling increase ($450M, new total $7.25B) means new task orders are coming. If you're on this vehicle, staff up your task order response team now.

> **The federal IT market is bifurcating — and that's good news for specialists.**
