## Substack Setup Instructions

### Step 1: Create the Email Sequence

1. Log into Substack at https://govconintelligence.substack.com
2. Go to **Settings → Email automation**
3. Create new automation: **"5-Day GovCon BD Intelligence Course"**
4. Set trigger: **"When someone subscribes via [course signup form]"**

### Step 2: Configure the Drip Schedule

- **Day 1:** Immediately upon signup (within 5 minutes)
- **Day 2:** 24 hours after signup
- **Day 3:** 48 hours after signup
- **Day 4:** 72 hours after signup
- **Day 5:** 96 hours after signup

**Note:** Use Substack's relative timing ("+1 day", "+2 days", etc.) rather than fixed send times. This ensures users receive emails at consistent intervals regardless of signup time.

### Step 3: Add Each Email to the Sequence

For each email:
1. Copy content from `day-N.md` file
2. Paste into Substack email editor
3. Format headers, bold text, and bullet points for readability
4. Preview on mobile (50% of BD professionals check email on phone)
5. Send test email to yourself
6. Activate in sequence

### Step 4: Create a Dedicated Signup Form

Create a **separate signup form** for the course (different from the main newsletter signup):

- **Form URL:** https://govconintelligence.substack.com/course-signup (or custom subdomain)
- **Form headline:** "5-Day GovCon BD Intelligence Course (Free)"
- **Form subheadline:** "Learn how to use public data for competitive intelligence. One lesson per day, delivered to your inbox."
- **Button text:** "Start the Course"

**Why separate form?** Allows you to:
- Track course conversions separately from direct newsletter signups
- Tag users as "course graduates" vs. "direct subscribers"
- Send different welcome emails based on entry point

### Step 5: Add Form to Landing Page

Add the course signup form to these locations:
1. **Homepage:** Above the fold, as a lead magnet CTA
2. **Sample Report Page:** After users read the sample, offer the course
3. **LinkedIn Posts:** Link to course signup (not generic newsletter signup)
4. **Email Signature:** "Take the free 5-Day BD Intelligence Course"

### Step 6: Tag Course Subscribers

In Substack automation settings:
- **Tag all course subscribers:** "Course: 5-Day BD Intel"
- **Tag course completers (Day 5 opened):** "Course: Completed"

This allows you to:
- Send follow-up campaigns to course graduates
- Exclude course subscribers from "intro to newsletter" campaigns
- Analyze conversion rates (course → active reader → paid subscriber)

### Step 7: Bridge to Weekly Newsletter

After Day 5 (96 hours post-signup), add subscribers to the **main weekly newsletter list**.

**Transition email (Day 6, optional):**
- Subject: "You completed the course. Here's what's next."
- Content: Congratulate them, remind them the newsletter arrives every Monday at 8 AM ET, set expectations for format and value.

**Alternative (simpler):** Skip the transition email. Day 5 already ends with the newsletter CTA. Just ensure they receive the next Monday's newsletter.

### Drop-off Analysis

Track where users drop off:
- **Day 1 → Day 2 drop-off:** Subject line or Day 1 content quality issue
- **Day 2 → Day 3 drop-off:** Content is too long or not actionable enough
- **Day 4 → Day 5 drop-off:** Users got what they needed, Day 5 pitch not compelling

**If completion rate <50%:** Shorten emails, increase actionability, improve Day 5 CTA.

## A/B Testing Strategy

Once you have 100+ course subscribers, run these tests:

### Test 1: Subject Line Variations (Weeks 3-4)
- **Control:** Current subject lines (value-focused)
- **Variant:** Curiosity-driven subject lines (e.g., "The recompete trick your competitors don't know")
- **Measure:** Open rate by variant

### Test 2: Email Length (Months 2-3)
- **Control:** Current emails (400-500 words)
- **Variant:** Shorter emails (250-300 words)
- **Measure:** Completion rate and newsletter conversion

### Test 3: CTA Placement (Months 3-4)
- **Control:** CTA at end of Day 5
- **Variant:** CTA at end of Day 3 AND Day 5 (double touch)
- **Measure:** Newsletter signup rate

### Test 4: Action Item Complexity (Months 4-6)
- **Control:** "Run this search" action items (require work)
- **Variant:** "Here's what we found" action items (passive learning)
- **Measure:** User feedback and engagement

## Technical Notes

### Deliverability Best Practices

- **Warm up your domain:** Send to small batches first (10, 25, 50 subscribers) before scaling to 100+
- **Monitor spam complaints:** If >0.1% mark as spam, pause and review content
- **Use plain text + light HTML:** Avoid heavy images, complex formatting (triggers spam filters)
- **Authenticate your domain:** Set up SPF, DKIM, DMARC records for govconintelligence.substack.com

### Unsubscribe Handling

- **Unsubscribe from course:** Remove from automation, but keep on newsletter list (they may want just the newsletter)
- **Unsubscribe from all:** Honor immediately, no "are you sure?" screens

### Data Privacy

- **CAN-SPAM compliant:** Include physical address, unsubscribe link in every email
- **GDPR compliant:** Add "I agree to receive emails" checkbox to signup form
- **Retention policy:** Delete email addresses of unsubscribed users after 30 days

## Questions & Troubleshooting

**Q: What if users don't complete the course?**
A: Acceptable. Even if they only read Day 1, they've learned a valuable technique. Send a "You're missing out" re-engagement email on Day 7 to non-completers.

**Q: Should we gate the course (require email signup for each lesson)?**
A: No. Once they sign up, deliver all 5 lessons automatically. Gating creates friction and reduces completion rates.

**Q: Can users binge the course (read all 5 emails at once)?**
A: Not via automation, but they could request it. Consider offering a "Download full course PDF" option for users who prefer to read at their own pace.

**Q: What if someone signs up mid-week? Do they wait until Monday for the newsletter?**
A: Yes. Day 5 email sets expectation: "See you Monday morning." This creates anticipation and ensures they receive the newsletter on schedule.

**Q: Should we offer a certificate of completion?**
A: Not for MVP. Consider for Phase 2 if users request it (LinkedIn-shareable badge, "GovCon BD Intelligence Certified" image).

## Success Indicators (First 30 Days)

**Green Lights (Keep Going):**
- 50+ course signups in Week 1
- 50%+ open rate on Day 1
- 60%+ completion rate (Day 5 opens)
- 80%+ newsletter conversion from completers
- 2+ unsolicited testimonials or "this was helpful" replies

**Yellow Lights (Iterate):**
- 25-50 signups (increase promotion effort)
- 40-50% open rate (test subject lines)
- 40-60% completion rate (shorten emails, increase actionability)
- 60-80% newsletter conversion (strengthen Day 5 CTA)

**Red Lights (Pivot):**
- <25 signups (audience doesn't want this, or promotion channels aren't working)
- <40% open rate (subject lines or content quality issue)
- <40% completion rate (content isn't valuable or too long)
- <60% newsletter conversion (newsletter value prop isn't compelling)

If you hit 2+ red lights, run user interviews with non-completers to diagnose the issue.
