# Day 1: How to Find Recompetes 6 Months Before SAM.gov

**Subject: Your first competitive advantage: Recompete intelligence**

Welcome to the 5-Day GovCon BD Intelligence Course. Over the next week, you'll learn how to use free public data to build a competitive intelligence system that most BD teams miss.

Today's lesson: How to find recompetes 6 months before they hit SAM.gov.

## The Problem: You're Already Too Late

By the time an RFP drops on SAM.gov, the incumbent has been positioning for 6+ months. They've shaped requirements, built relationships with the program office, and locked in teaming partners.

You're playing catch-up from day one.

## The Solution: USAspending.gov Contract End Dates

Most BD professionals don't know this: USAspending.gov tracks contract end dates for every federal award. This is your early warning system.

**Here's how it works:**

1. Go to USAspending.gov → Advanced Search
2. Filter by your target agency (e.g., "Air Force")
3. Filter by NAICS code (e.g., 541512 - Computer Systems Design)
4. Add "Current End Date" filter → Set range to 6-12 months from today
5. Export to CSV

You now have a list of every contract expiring in your sweet spot, complete with:
- Incumbent company name
- Contract value
- Award history
- Program office contact info (sometimes)

## Real Example: $4.5M DHS Cybersecurity Contract

In March 2026, a smart BD manager at an 8(a) firm used this technique to find a DHS cybersecurity support contract ending in October 2026. The incumbent was a mid-tier prime with a mediocre CPAR.

She started positioning in April:
- Reached out to the program manager for a "market research" call
- Built a teaming relationship with a small business in the same building as DHS
- Drafted a capability statement specifically addressing gaps in the incumbent's performance

By the time the RFP dropped in August, she was the favorite. She won the recompete in November.

**Her edge? Six months of lead time.**

## Your Action Item (10 Minutes)

Pick ONE agency and ONE NAICS code where you compete. Run the USAspending search for contracts ending in the next 9-12 months. Download the CSV.

You'll likely find 5-10 opportunities that aren't on your radar yet.

Pick the top 2 and add them to your pipeline. Start researching incumbents this week.

## Why This Matters

Most BD teams are reactive. They wait for SAM.gov alerts and chase RFPs with 30-day deadlines.

The best BD teams are proactive. They identify recompetes 6+ months early and shape the battlefield before the RFP drops.

This one technique will change how you do pipeline development.
