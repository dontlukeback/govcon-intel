# Day 2: Track Your Competitors' Federal Wins in 10 Minutes

**Subject: Competitive intelligence most BD teams ignore**

Yesterday you learned how to find recompetes early using USAspending contract end dates. Today: How to track your competitors' wins (and losses) to spot opportunities before they show up on SAM.gov.

## The Problem: You're Flying Blind

Most BD teams can name their top 3-5 competitors, but they don't systematically track them.

Questions you should be able to answer (but probably can't):
- Which agencies is your competitor winning at?
- Are they moving into new verticals?
- Did they just lose a major recompete? (vulnerability signal)
- Are they winning more 8(a) set-asides or unrestricted?

This intelligence tells you where to compete, where to avoid, and when to strike.

## The Solution: USAspending Recipient Search

USAspending.gov lets you search by recipient name. This is your competitor tracking system.

**Here's the 10-minute workflow:**

1. Go to USAspending.gov → Advanced Search
2. Click "Recipient" filter
3. Enter competitor company name (e.g., "Booz Allen Hamilton")
4. Set time range to "Last 12 months"
5. Sort by award date (newest first)
6. Export to CSV

You now have a full list of every federal contract they've won in the past year.

## Real Example: 8(a) Competitor Expanding Into Cloud

In January 2026, a BD manager noticed her 8(a) competitor had won 4 cloud infrastructure contracts in Q4 2025 — up from zero the previous year.

She dug deeper:
- All 4 awards were with DoD agencies (Army, Navy, Air Force)
- Contract sizes: $800K - $2.5M
- All were FedRAMP-related support services

**Her insight:** The competitor hired a cloud architect in September 2025 and was pivoting into cloud. They were now a direct threat on every DoD cloud RFP.

She adjusted her teaming strategy immediately — on the next 3 cloud opportunities, she partnered with a different 8(a) firm to block them out.

## What to Look For (The "Competitor Health Check")

When you pull a competitor's awards, scan for these signals:

**Growth signals (they're getting stronger):**
- Increasing award frequency (winning more often)
- Larger contract values (moving upmarket)
- New agencies (diversifying customer base)
- Prime awards vs. subcontracts (becoming less dependent on primes)

**Vulnerability signals (time to attack):**
- Declining award frequency (dry pipeline)
- Lost incumbencies (check by comparing to previous years)
- Geographic expansion (spreading themselves thin)
- Heavy concentration in one agency (risky if that agency cuts budget)

## Your Action Item (10 Minutes)

Pick your #1 competitor. Run the USAspending recipient search for the last 12 months. Download the CSV.

Ask yourself:
1. Are they winning more or less than last year?
2. Which agencies are they targeting?
3. Did they lose any incumbencies I should chase?
4. Are they moving into new verticals where I compete?

Add this to your calendar as a monthly recurring task. Competitive intelligence is perishable — run this every 30 days.

## Why This Matters

Most BD teams react to RFPs. The best BD teams study competitors and predict where they'll compete next.

When you track competitors systematically, you can:
- Avoid competing where they're dominant (save your win rate)
- Attack when they're vulnerable (pursue their lost incumbencies)
- Pre-empt them on teaming (lock up key subs before they do)

This is how mid-tier primes do competitive intelligence. Now you can too.
