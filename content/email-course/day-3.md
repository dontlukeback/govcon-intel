# Day 3: The 3 NAICS Codes Every IT Services Firm Should Monitor

**Subject: You're probably tracking the wrong NAICS codes**

Yesterday you learned how to track competitors using USAspending recipient search. Today: How to identify the NAICS codes with the highest win probability for your firm (hint: it's probably not the ones you're tracking).

## The Problem: NAICS Overload

There are 50+ NAICS codes related to IT services. Most firms monitor 10-15 codes and end up drowning in irrelevant opportunities.

Common mistake: Adding every NAICS code remotely related to your capabilities. This creates noise, not signal.

## The Solution: Data-Driven NAICS Analysis

Instead of guessing which NAICS codes to track, let the data tell you.

**Here's the 15-minute workflow:**

1. Go to USAspending.gov → Advanced Search
2. Filter by agency (pick your target agency, e.g., "Air Force")
3. Filter by contract type (e.g., "Services")
4. Filter by award date (last 12 months)
5. Export to CSV
6. Open in Excel → Pivot table by NAICS code
7. Sort by "Total Contract Value" and "Number of Awards"

You now have a ranked list of NAICS codes by spending volume in your target market.

## Real Example: Cybersecurity Firm's NAICS Strategy

A small 8(a) cybersecurity firm was monitoring 12 NAICS codes. They were getting 200+ SAM.gov alerts per week but only bidding on 2-3 opportunities per quarter.

Their BD manager ran the analysis above for DoD agencies. Here's what she found:

**Top 3 NAICS codes by contract value (FY2025):**
1. **541512** (Computer Systems Design Services) - $12.4B
2. **541519** (Other Computer Related Services) - $8.7B
3. **541690** (Other Scientific and Technical Consulting) - $6.2B

**Surprise finding:** NAICS 541330 (Engineering Services) had 3x more awards than 541512, but 80% were <$100K (not worth pursuing for her firm).

She narrowed her monitoring to just **541512** and **541519**, focusing on awards $500K-$5M.

Result: 60% reduction in noise, 40% increase in bid rate.

## The "Magic Three" for Most IT Services Firms

If you're in IT services (cloud, cybersecurity, DevSecOps, data analytics), 90% of relevant opportunities fall into these 3 codes:

**541512 - Computer Systems Design Services**
- Custom software development
- IT architecture and design
- Systems integration
- Network design

**541519 - Other Computer Related Services**
- Cybersecurity services
- IT support and help desk
- Cloud migration and operations
- Data analytics and visualization

**541611 - Administrative Management and General Management Consulting Services**
- IT strategy and planning
- Program management support
- Business process improvement with IT component

**Pro tip:** If your firm is 8(a) or small business, add filters for set-asides. Don't waste time tracking full-and-open competitions where primes dominate.

## Your Action Item (15 Minutes)

Run the USAspending analysis for your target agency:
1. Export all service contracts from the last 12 months
2. Pivot by NAICS code
3. Identify the top 3 codes by total contract value in your sweet spot ($500K-$10M, or whatever your range is)
4. Update your SAM.gov alerts to ONLY track those 3 codes

Be ruthless. More NAICS codes = more noise. Focus on the 3 that drive 80% of the value.

## Why This Matters

Most BD teams set up SAM.gov alerts once and never revisit them. They end up with alert fatigue — 100+ emails per week, 95% irrelevant.

The best BD teams use data to focus. They monitor fewer NAICS codes but track them more aggressively.

When you focus on the right 3 NAICS codes, you can:
- Spend less time filtering noise
- Spend more time researching high-probability opportunities
- Increase your bid rate (because you're only seeing relevant opps)
- Improve your win rate (because you're not spreading yourself thin)

This is the difference between reactive BD (chasing everything) and strategic BD (chasing the right things).
