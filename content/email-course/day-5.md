## The Problem: Intelligence Goes Stale

You run the USAspending searches once, get excited, and then... life happens. Proposals pile up, customer meetings fill your calendar, and 3 months later you realize you haven't checked for recompetes in 90 days.

Intelligence is only valuable if it's **fresh** and **actionable**.

## The Solution: Monday Morning Intelligence Review (10 Minutes)

Here's the weekly routine used by the best BD professionals:

### Monday, 8:00 AM (Before your standup)

**Minutes 1-3: Recompete Scan**
- Pull up your saved USAspending search (contracts ending in 6-12 months, your NAICS codes)
- Scan for new contracts that entered the "expiring soon" window since last Monday
- Add top 2-3 to your pipeline for research this week

**Minutes 4-6: Competitor Check**
- Pick ONE competitor (rotate through your top 5 weekly)
- Run USAspending recipient search for last 30 days
- Note any new wins, lost incumbencies, or vertical shifts
- Flag anything that impacts your positioning

**Minutes 7-8: NAICS Trend Review**
- Check USAspending for your top 3 NAICS codes (last 7 days)
- Sort by contract value (high to low)
- Scan for large awards that signal market shifts (e.g., sudden surge in Zero Trust contracts)

**Minutes 9-10: Pipeline Prioritization**
- Review your active pipeline against the "Is It Wired?" framework
- De-prioritize anything with 3+ red flags
- Double down on opportunities where you learned something new this week (e.g., incumbent just lost another contract = vulnerability)

That's it. **10 minutes, every Monday, before your first meeting.**

## Real Example: How This Saved a $2M Opportunity

A BD manager at an 8(a) firm ran this workflow every Monday for 8 weeks. In Week 6, she noticed:

**Recompete Scan:** Army cyber support contract ($2.3M) ending in 7 months
**Competitor Check:** The incumbent just lost a different Army contract (CPAR issues)
**Wired Check:** Industry day scheduled, program office responsive to emails (not wired)

She started positioning immediately:
- Attended industry day (met program manager)
- Partnered with a small business that had better past performance
- Drafted technical approach addressing the incumbent's CPAR weaknesses

When the RFP dropped 5 months later, she was the favorite. She won in Q1 2026.

**Her edge:** She found the opportunity in Week 6 (7 months early) and had 20+ weeks to position.
