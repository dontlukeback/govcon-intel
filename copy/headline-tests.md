# Hero Headline A/B Test Candidates

**Current Headline:** "Stop Missing Federal Contracts"
**Current Subhead:** "Weekly intelligence on who's winning, who's losing, and where the recompetes are. Free to start. No GovWin contract required."

**Test Period:** March 18 - April 1, 2026 (2 weeks)
**Success Metric:** Email signup conversion rate (target: 3-5%)

---

## TEST A: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

### Framework
**Problem:** State the pain point clearly
**Agitate:** Make them feel the cost of not solving it
**Solution:** Present your product as the relief

### Headline
"You're Missing $487M in Recompetes"

### Subhead
"Contracts in NAICS 541512 and 541519 expire Q3 2026. By the time solicitations hit SAM.gov, incumbents already have 6 months of capture work done. Know about recompetes before they're posted."

### Rationale
- **Specific number** ($487M) stops the scroll—it's real data from our pipeline
- **Specific NAICS codes** signal domain expertise (we speak their language)
- **Specific timeframe** (Q3 2026) creates urgency without false scarcity
- **Problem → Agitation → Solution** structure:
  - Problem: You're missing recompetes
  - Agitate: Incumbents have 6-month head start
  - Solution: Know before they're posted

### Why This Might Win
- Most **specific** of all three options (real number, real NAICS, real quarter)
- Appeals to **loss aversion** (missing money is stronger motivator than gaining money)
- **Urgency without manipulation** (Q3 2026 is real, not artificial countdown timer)

### Risk
- **Only relevant to IT services firms** in NAICS 541512/541519 (might exclude other verticals)
- **Number could feel too big** ($487M across hundreds of contracts—not $487M per contract)

### Best For
Small/mid-size IT services contractors actively bidding in NAICS 541512/541519

---

## TEST B: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

### Framework
**Attention:** Hook that stops the scroll
**Interest:** Problem they recognize
**Desire:** Solution that solves it
**Action:** Specific CTA

### Headline
"Track Every Recompete in Your NAICS"

### Subhead
"Contracts expire every week—DOD, VA, DOE, State. Most firms find out 6 months too late. Get weekly intel on recompetes, new awards, and competitor wins before solicitations drop. Free Monday briefings."

### Rationale
- **Benefit-first headline** (track recompetes) not problem-first (stop missing)
- **Desire framing:** Paints the picture of what they GET (weekly intel, competitor wins, free briefings)
- **AIDA structure:**
  - Attention: "Track every recompete"
  - Interest: "Contracts expire every week—DOD, VA, DOE, State"
  - Desire: "Get weekly intel... before solicitations drop"
  - Action: Implicit (sign up to get this)

### Why This Might Win
- **Positive framing** (track everything) vs. negative framing (stop missing)
- **Broader appeal** (not limited to specific NAICS codes)
- **Lists agencies** (DOD, VA, DOE, State) to show coverage breadth

### Risk
- **Less specific** than Test A (no dollar amounts, no specific NAICS)
- **"Every recompete in your NAICS"** is a big promise—can we deliver?

### Best For
Broader GovCon audience across multiple verticals who want comprehensive coverage

---

## TEST C: BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

### Framework
**Before:** Life without the product (pain, wasted time, missed opportunities)
**After:** Life with the product (results, savings, wins)
**Bridge:** The product is how you get from Before to After

### Headline
"From SAM.gov Drudgery to Monday Morning Intel"

### Subhead
"Before: You're checking SAM.gov daily, chasing stale leads, finding out about recompetes after incumbents already started capture. After: You get a weekly brief with recompetes expiring in 6-18 months, competitor wins, and plain-English analysis. Every Monday. Free."

### Rationale
- **Transformation story** (Before → After)
- **Specific pain point** (SAM.gov drudgery) every GovCon BD person feels
- **BAB structure:**
  - Before: SAM.gov drudgery, stale leads, finding out too late
  - After: Weekly brief, 6-18 month lead time, competitor intel, free
  - Bridge: Implicit (sign up to get from Before to After)

### Why This Might Win
- **Relatable pain point** (SAM.gov drudgery) everyone in GovCon knows
- **Clear transformation** (drudgery → organized intel)
- **Emphasizes time savings** (no more daily checking)

### Risk
- **Headline is clever, not clear** ("SAM.gov Drudgery" might require context)
- **Longer subhead** (might lose attention before CTA)
- **"Free" at the end might feel buried**

### Best For
BD professionals who manually check SAM.gov daily and feel the pain of that process

## RECOMMENDATION: Which to Test First

### First Test: **Test A (PAS)**

**Why:**
1. **Most specific** (real number, real NAICS, real quarter)
2. **Loss aversion is stronger** than gain motivation in B2B
3. **Easy to measure success** (if this doesn't convert, the others won't either)

**Test Setup:**
- 50% of traffic sees Test A
- 50% of traffic sees Current Headline
- Run for 2 weeks (March 18 - April 1)
- Measure: email signup conversion rate

**Success Criteria:**
- If Test A converts **20% better** than Current → declare winner, deploy permanently
- If Test A converts **within 10%** of Current → test is inconclusive, try Test B
- If Test A converts **worse** than Current → revert, try Test B

---

### Second Test: **Test B (AIDA)**

**When to run:** If Test A is inconclusive or performs worse

**Why:**
1. **Broader appeal** (not limited to specific NAICS codes)
2. **Positive framing** might resonate better with audience tired of fear-based marketing
3. **Lists agencies** (DOD, VA, DOE, State) to show breadth

**Test Setup:**
- 50% Test B vs. 50% Current
- Run for 2 weeks
- Same success criteria as Test A

---

### Third Test: **Test C (BAB)** — Only if Tests A and B fail

**Why last:**
1. **Clever headline** is risky (might confuse instead of convert)
2. **Longer copy** in subhead (attention span risk)
3. **Transformation story** works better in case studies/emails than landing page hero

**Test Setup:**
- Only run if both Test A and Test B underperform Current
- Might work better as email subject line (A/B test there first)

## TESTING PROTOCOL

### Setup
1. Use Beehiiv's built-in A/B testing (if available) or Vercel/Netlify split testing
2. 50/50 traffic split
3. Track: **email signup conversion rate** (primary) and **time on page** (secondary)

### Duration
- Minimum: 2 weeks per test
- Minimum traffic: 1,000 visitors per variant (2,000 total)
- If traffic is lower, extend test duration

### Decision Framework
| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Winner converts 20%+ better | Deploy permanently, test next element |
| Winner converts 10-20% better | Extend test another week to confirm |
| Winner converts <10% better | Test is inconclusive, try next variant |
| Variant performs worse | Revert to control, try next variant |

### What to Test Next (After Headline)
1. **CTA button text:** "Get This Week's Brief" vs. "Track Your NAICS" vs. "See the Report"
2. **Hero subhead length:** Short (1 sentence) vs. Medium (2 sentences) vs. Long (3 sentences)
3. **Social proof placement:** Above fold vs. Below problem section vs. No social proof
4. **Pricing section:** Free only vs. Free + paid tier preview vs. Free + waitlist for paid
