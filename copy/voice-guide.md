# Brand Voice — GovCon Weekly Intelligence

## Who We Are (in one sentence)
The intelligence analyst who cuts through federal contract noise and tells you exactly which opportunities to chase and why.

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Direct
We say what we mean. No hedging, no corporate speak, no fluff.

**Do:**
- "You're missing contracts worth $14M in your NAICS."
- "Three recompetes expire in Q3. Start capture now."
- "Leidos picked up $663M at DOT this week. You should know about this."

**Don't:**
- "Some organizations may experience suboptimal opportunity identification."
- "There appears to be potential for enhanced capture planning activities."
- "It might be beneficial to consider exploring relevant solicitations."

### 2. Expert
We know this domain cold. We use the right terminology because we belong here.

**Do:**
- "NAICS 541512 is up 28% week-over-week."
- "VA exercised option year 3 on the IDIQ ceiling."
- "Set-aside: SDVOSB. Vehicle: GSA Schedule 70."
- "PoP ends Sep 2026. Incumbent: Booz Allen."

**Don't:**
- "There's been some growth in the computer services area."
- "The government bought more stuff from veterans this week."
- "Some big companies got contracts."

### 3. Actionable
Everything we say leads to something you can do. No insight without action.

**Do:**
- "Submit a capabilities brief to the CO by Friday."
- "Recommend teaming outreach to incumbent subs now."
- "Track this contractor's next three wins for pattern recognition."

**Don't:**
- "Consider exploring potential engagement strategies."
- "This may warrant further investigation and analysis."
- "It could be useful to monitor the competitive landscape."

### 4. Confident
We don't apologize for knowing things. We state facts, signal patterns, and make calls.

**Do:**
- "This is the most active week for cybersecurity awards in 6 months."
- "DOE dominated by dollar value at $3.5B."
- "Small business set-asides were thin—only 7 of 1,173 awards."

**Don't:**
- "It seems like there might be more cybersecurity activity lately."
- "DOE possibly had some larger contracts this week."
- "Small business opportunities may be limited at this time."

## Vocabulary

### Words We Use
- **Recompete** (not "re-compete" or "renewal")
- **Ceiling** (contract value cap)
- **PoP** (Period of Performance)
- **IDIQ** (Indefinite Delivery, Indefinite Quantity)
- **Set-aside** (8(a), SDVOSB, WOSB, HUBZone)
- **NAICS** (North American Industry Classification System)
- **Vehicle** (GSA Schedule, GWAC, BPA)
- **Incumbent** (current contract holder)
- **Option year** (not "renewal")
- **Capture** (not "business development")
- **CO** (Contracting Officer)
- **Mod** (contract modification)
- **Teaming** (not "partnership")
- **Prime** (prime contractor)
- **Sub** (subcontractor)

### Words We Never Use
- "Synergy"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "Solutions" (unless it's in a contract title)
- "Ecosystem"
- "Thought leader"
- "Best practices"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Circle back"
- "Touch base"
- "Move the needle"
- "Game-changer"
- "Disruptive" (unless talking about cybersecurity)

### Language Precision
- **Use:** "Track your competitors' wins"
- **Not:** "Monitor competitive intelligence"

- **Use:** "$14.2M ceiling"
- **Not:** "A contract worth approximately fourteen million dollars"

- **Use:** "PoP ends Sep 2026"
- **Not:** "The performance period concludes in the third quarter of fiscal year 2026"

- **Use:** "Booz Allen is the incumbent"
- **Not:** "Booz Allen Hamilton currently holds this engagement"

## Tone Shifts by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Newsletter headline** | Urgent, specific | "547 cybersecurity awards this week. VA led with $820M." |
| **Recompete alert** | Direct, deadline-focused | "PoP ends Sep 2026. Six-month capture window opens now." |
| **Competitor intel** | Factual, strategic | "Leidos exercised option year 3 on DHA Health IT—$22M through FY27." |
| **Analyst take** | Expert, actionable | "VA IT modernization spend up 18% YoY. Recommend teaming outreach to incumbent subs now." |
| **Email subject line** | Specific, benefit-first | "3 recompetes in NAICS 541512 expire Q3 2026" |
| **Sales email** | Personal, casual | "Thought you might find this useful for your capture pipeline." |
| **Error message** | Helpful, brief | "Couldn't load this week's report. Refresh or email us at govconweekly@gmail.com." |
| **Legal/Privacy** | Clear, formal | "We collect your email address to deliver the weekly intelligence brief." |
| **Social media** | Expert but approachable | "$663M to Leidos at DOT this week. If they're your competitor, you should know about this before your next capture meeting." |

## Writing Rules

### Headlines
- Lead with the number: "$14M VA recompete expires Q3"
- Not: "Important opportunity at the VA"

### Lists
- Start with the action or outcome: "Track competitor wins in your NAICS"
- Not: "Competitor tracking is available"

### Contract Details
Always include: Agency + Value + Incumbent/Winner + NAICS + PoP/Date
- "Dept. of Veterans Affairs | $14.2M ceiling | Incumbent: Booz Allen | NAICS 541512 | PoP ends Sep 2026"

### Email/Newsletter Structure
1. **Subject:** Specific number or signal
2. **First line:** The most important thing
3. **Body:** Facts → Pattern → Action
4. **Close:** One clear next step

### What We Never Do
- Bury the lead (put the most important thing first)
- Use passive voice ("Reports are generated" → "You get a report")
- Write "We" when we mean "You" ("We built a tool" → "You get weekly intel")
- Make vague claims ("Better intelligence" → "Track 547 cybersecurity awards/week")
- Hide behind qualifiers ("may," "might," "could," "possibly")

## Examples: Before/After

### Landing Page Headline
**Before:** "Federal Contract Intelligence Platform"
**After:** "Stop Missing Federal Contracts"

**Why:** Benefit beats category. "Stop missing" is the outcome they want.

---

### Newsletter Intro
**Before:** "This week's newsletter provides valuable insights into the federal contracting landscape."
**After:** "547 cybersecurity awards this week worth $1.2B. Here's what matters for your pipeline."

**Why:** Specific numbers, immediate value, outcome-focused.

---

### Recompete Alert
**Before:** "A contract opportunity may be available soon at the Department of Veterans Affairs for IT modernization services. The current contract holder is Booz Allen Hamilton."
**After:** "VA IT Modernization Support Services | $14.2M ceiling | Incumbent: Booz Allen | NAICS 541512 | PoP ends Sep 2026 | 6-month capture window"

**Why:** Structured data format, scannable, all key facts in one line.

---

### Call to Action
**Before:** "Learn More"
**After:** "Get This Week's Brief"

**Why:** Specific outcome. "Learn More" tells me nothing.

---

### Competitor Intel
**Before:** "Leidos has been awarded a contract by the Department of Transportation for the ATOP program. This represents a significant win for the company."
**After:** "$663M to Leidos at DOT this week—ATOP program. If they're your competitor, you should know about this."

**Why:** Lead with the number, add strategic context, make it personal.

## Copy Quality Checklist

Before any copy goes live, verify:

- [ ] **Headline test:** Would a BD director stop scrolling for this?
- [ ] **"So what?" test:** After every sentence, does the reader know why they should care?
- [ ] **Jargon check:** Are we using GovCon terminology correctly? (Good jargon = domain expertise)
- [ ] **Benefit check:** Are we selling the outcome, not the feature?
- [ ] **CTA check:** Does the reader know exactly what to do next?
- [ ] **Read-aloud test:** Does it sound natural spoken out loud?
- [ ] **Cut test:** Can I remove 20% of the words without losing meaning?
- [ ] **Specificity test:** Are there specific numbers, NAICS codes, dollar amounts, or company names?
- [ ] **Voice check:** Does this sound like an intelligence analyst, not a marketing agency?
- [ ] **Truth check:** Is every claim accurate and defensible with public data?

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Newsletter (Beehiiv)
- **Subject line:** Under 50 characters, lead with a number
  - Good: "547 cybersecurity awards this week—$1.2B"
  - Bad: "This week's GovCon newsletter is here!"
- **Preview text:** Expand on subject, don't repeat it
  - Subject: "547 cybersecurity awards this week—$1.2B"
  - Preview: "VA led with $820M. DOE dominated by dollar value. Here's what matters for your pipeline."
- **First line:** Most important signal of the week
- **Structure:** Top Awards → Recompetes → Competitor Intel → Analyst Take
- **Length:** 800-1,200 words (3-5 minute read)
- **CTAs:** One per section max (don't split attention)

### LinkedIn Posts
- **Hook in first 2 lines** (that's all they see before "...see more")
  - Good: "$663M to Leidos at DOT this week.\nIf they're your competitor, you need to know about this."
  - Bad: "Happy Monday! Here's this week's federal contracting update..."
- **Line breaks between paragraphs** (LinkedIn collapses walls of text)
- **Use bullets with →** (more visual than plain dashes)
- **End with a question or CTA** to drive comments
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 max
  - #GovCon #FederalContracting #GovernmentContracts #NAICS541512 #Cybersecurity
- **Optimal length:** 150-300 words

### Email (Sales/Outreach)
- **Subject:** Specific and personal
  - Good: "3 recompetes in your NAICS expire Q3"
  - Bad: "You should check out GovCon Weekly"
- **First sentence:** Make them want to read the second sentence
  - Good: "You're tracking cybersecurity recompetes at VA, right?"
  - Bad: "I wanted to reach out and introduce our platform..."
- **One CTA per email**
- **P.S. line:** Second most-read part of any email
  - Use it: "P.S. This week's report tracked 547 cybersecurity awards. Want early access?"
- **Plain text > HTML** for trust in cold outreach

### Social Media (Twitter/X)
- **Lead with the number:** "$663M to Leidos at DOT"
- **Add context in thread:** Why it matters, what to do about it
- **Use line breaks** for readability
- **No exclamation marks** (looks unprofessional in B2B)
- **Include link** to newsletter signup or specific report

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### 1. Corporate Speak
**Bad:** "We are pleased to announce that our platform provides enhanced visibility into the federal procurement ecosystem."
**Good:** "Track 547 cybersecurity awards per week. Know who's winning before your competitors do."

### 2. Feature Dump
**Bad:** "Our platform includes recompete tracking, new award monitoring, competitor intelligence, and strategic analysis."
**Good:** "Never miss a recompete in your NAICS. Track your competitors' wins. Get plain-English analysis every Monday."

### 3. Vague CTAs
**Bad:** "Learn More" | "Sign Up" | "Get Started"
**Good:** "Get This Week's Brief" | "Track Your NAICS" | "See the Report"

### 4. Burying the Lead
**Bad:** "This week, we analyzed federal contract data and identified several interesting trends across multiple verticals. One area that showed particular activity was cybersecurity..."
**Good:** "547 cybersecurity awards this week—most active week in 6 months. Here's what matters."

### 5. Passive Voice
**Bad:** "Reports are generated weekly and delivered to your inbox."
**Good:** "You get a report every Monday morning."

### 6. "We" Language
**Bad:** "We built a tool that tracks federal contracts."
**Good:** "You track every contract in your NAICS without checking SAM.gov manually."

### 7. Qualifier Hedging
**Bad:** "This could potentially indicate that there might be increased activity in the cybersecurity sector."
**Good:** "Cybersecurity awards are up 28% week-over-week."

## Voice Consistency Tests

If you're unsure whether copy fits the brand voice, ask:

1. **Would an intelligence analyst say this?** (Not a marketer, not a salesperson—an analyst)
2. **Does it lead with data?** (Numbers, names, dates, NAICS codes)
3. **Is there a clear action?** (What should the reader do with this information?)
4. **Would a BD director forward this to their team?** (Is it useful enough to share?)
5. **Can I remove any words without losing meaning?** (If yes, remove them)
