## Break-Even Analysis

**Fixed Costs:** $300/month
**Average Variable Cost per Paid User:** ~$5/month (blended)
**Average Revenue per Paid User:** ~$200/month (blended, 70% Pro / 30% Enterprise)

**Break-Even Subscribers:** $300 / ($200 - $5) = **1.5 subscribers**

**Key Insight:** We break even with 2 paid subscribers. Every subscriber after that is pure profit.

**Why This Matters:**
- No need for funding
- Can bootstrap forever
- High margin = high experimentation budget (can afford to try things)

## Sensitivity Analysis

**What if free-to-paid conversion is only 2%?**
- Base Case MRR drops from $129K to $52K (still profitable, just slower)
- Need to focus on conversion optimization (better onboarding, more free tier value)

**What if churn is 10% instead of 5%?**
- Base Case MRR drops from $129K to $78K (still profitable, but much slower growth)
- Need to focus on retention (better content, customer success)

**What if Pro pricing is $79 instead of $99?**
- Revenue drops 20%, but conversion might increase 30%
- Net effect: +4% revenue (worth testing)

**What if we raise Enterprise pricing to $999?**
- Revenue increases 100% for Enterprise tier
- But likely reduces conversion by 60% (price sensitivity)
- Net effect: -20% revenue (don't do it)

**What if we add a paid acquisition channel (Google Ads at $50 CAC)?**
- At $99/month Pro tier, payback period = 1 month (if 5% churn)
- LTV = $1,980, CAC = $50, LTV:CAC = 40:1 (amazing)
- **Recommendation:** Test paid acquisition after Month 6 if organic growth slows

## Financial Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: Slow Free Subscriber Growth
**Impact:** Delayed paid subscriber growth (smaller top of funnel)
**Probability:** Medium
**Mitigation:**
- Double down on LinkedIn content (daily posts, engagement)
- Launch referral program (give 1 month free Pro for every referral)
- Guest post on GovCon blogs (ExecutiveBiz, FedScoop)

### Risk 2: Low Free-to-Paid Conversion
**Impact:** Revenue below projections despite large free base
**Probability:** Medium
**Mitigation:**
- A/B test pricing ($79 vs $99 vs $129)
- Improve free tier value (prove credibility)
- Add conversion triggers (email drip campaign, scarcity, social proof)

### Risk 3: High Churn
**Impact:** Revenue growth stalls (leaky bucket)
**Probability:** Low (if product delivers value)
**Mitigation:**
- Customer feedback surveys at 30/60/90 days
- Proactive customer success (email check-ins)
- Add features that increase stickiness (saved searches, custom alerts)

### Risk 4: Competitor Launches Similar Product
**Impact:** Slowed growth, price pressure
**Probability:** Medium (GovWin, Bloomberg could do this)
**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage (build brand, lock in customers)
- Better AI prompts (proprietary insights = moat)
- Faster iteration (we're nimble, they're slow)

### Risk 5: AI Costs Spike
**Impact:** Margin compression (OpenAI raises prices)
**Probability:** Low
**Mitigation:**
- Lock in OpenAI credits at current pricing
- Optimize prompts to reduce token usage
- Evaluate open-source models (Llama, Claude alternatives)
