## Competitive Landscape Analysis

### Tier 1: Enterprise Intelligence Platforms ($20K-100K/year)

#### GovWin IQ (Deltek)
- **Price:** ~$25,000-60,000/year per seat (based on industry reports)
- **Target:** Large primes (Lockheed, Northrop, Booz Allen)
- **Features:** Full federal contract database, pipeline management, CRM integration, team collaboration
- **Positioning:** "The system of record for federal BD teams"
- **Weakness:** Overkill for firms under $50M revenue. Requires dedicated analyst to extract value.

#### Bloomberg Government
- **Price:** ~$25,000-50,000/year per seat
- **Target:** Large contractors + government affairs teams
- **Features:** Contract data + legislative tracking + policy analysis + news
- **Positioning:** "Bloomberg Terminal for government"
- **Weakness:** Built for policy wonks, not BD practitioners. Too broad (covers legislation, not just contracts).

#### FedScoop/SAM.gov Premium Tools
- **Price:** $10,000-30,000/year
- **Target:** Mid-size contractors
- **Features:** Enhanced search, alerts, opportunity tracking
- **Positioning:** Enhanced versions of free government data
- **Weakness:** Data dump without intelligence. You still have to analyze it yourself.

### Tier 2: BD Tools & Alerts ($2K-10K/year)

#### GovTribe
- **Price:** ~$3,000-8,000/year
- **Target:** Small/mid contractors
- **Features:** Contract search, opportunity tracking, teaming partner discovery
- **Positioning:** "Affordable federal market intelligence"
- **Weakness:** Better search interface than SAM.gov, but still just filtered data, not insights.

#### onPoint (Shipley Associates)
- **Price:** ~$5,000-12,000/year
- **Target:** BD professionals
- **Features:** Capture management, proposal library, opportunity tracking
- **Positioning:** "Proposal/capture tool that includes federal data"
- **Weakness:** Focused on proposal management, not market intelligence.

### Tier 3: Free Government Data

#### SAM.gov / USAspending.gov
- **Price:** Free
- **Target:** Everyone
- **Features:** Raw federal contract data, opportunity postings
- **Positioning:** Official government transparency portals
- **Weakness:** Data overload. No analysis, no context, no "so what."

## Pricing Strategy & Tiers

**Price:** $0/month
**Goal:** Lead generation + brand building

**What's Included:**
- Weekly email newsletter (Sunday night)
- Executive summary (2-3 paragraphs)
- Top 3 urgent recompetes with action items
- 1 "Agency Strategy Signal" (where money is flowing)
- Social media one-liners for LinkedIn sharing
- Link to full report (upgrade CTA)

**Why Free:**
- Builds trust and demonstrates value
- Viral distribution (social sharing)
- Low-friction entry for skeptical buyers
- Allows us to build 10K+ subscriber base for credibility

**Conversion Hypothesis:** 5-10% of free subscribers convert to paid after 4-8 weeks.

### PRO TIER: "Full Intelligence Report"
**Price:** $99/month or $990/year (save $198)
**Target:** Small GovCon firms, independent BD consultants, capture managers

**What's Included (everything in Free, plus):**
- Full weekly intelligence report (7 sections, ~5000 words)
- All recompete analyses (10-15 per week)
- Agency strategy signals (15-20 agencies)
- Contractor power rankings (top 25)
- Small business spotlight + subcontracting opportunities
- Trend analysis & forward look
- Detailed action items with SAM.gov alert templates

**Why $99/month:**
- **10x cheaper than GovWin IQ** ($25K/year = $2,083/month)
- **Same price as a good B2B SaaS** (comparable to Ahrefs, SEMrush for SEO professionals)
- **Monthly coffee budget** for a BD team ($99 = 3 fancy coffees per person)
- **Low enough to expense without approval** at most firms
- **High enough to filter serious users** from tire-kickers

**Unit Economics:**
- Marginal cost per subscriber: ~$2/month (API calls, email delivery, hosting)
- Gross margin: 98%
- Payback period: <1 month (zero CAC if organic/referral)

**Ideal Customer Profile:**
- Small GovCon firms ($2M-50M revenue)
- 1-3 person BD teams
- Focused on DoD/VA/DHS tech contracts
- Currently using free SAM.gov + manual research
- Spends 5-10 hours/week tracking opportunities
- Values time savings at $50-150/hour

**Value Proposition Math:**
- Saves 3 hours/week of manual research = 12 hours/month
- At $75/hour (mid-level BD analyst) = $900/month value
- ROI: 9x

## Strategic Pricing Principles

### 1. Price for the Buyer, Not the Value
- **Value delivered:** $900/month (time savings)
- **Price charged:** $99/month
- **Ratio:** 9:1 value-to-price ratio
- **Why:** Lower price = faster adoption, viral growth, larger TAM

### 2. Land-and-Expand Model
- **Land:** Free tier (build audience)
- **Expand:** Pro tier (monetize engaged users)
- **Expand:** Enterprise tier (upsell teams/data access)
- **Future:** Custom integrations, consulting, events

### 3. Pricing as Positioning
- **Under $100/month:** Impulse purchase, no approval needed
- **Under $500/month:** VP approval, 1-2 week sales cycle
- **Over $500/month:** C-level approval, 1-3 month sales cycle
- **Our choice:** Stay under thresholds to maximize velocity

### 4. Pricing as Product
- Free tier is the best marketing (proves value before asking for money)
- Pro tier is the wedge (gets them in the door)
- Enterprise tier is the revenue driver (higher ARPU, stickier)

### 5. Resist the Urge to Go Upmarket Too Fast
- **Trap:** Chase $2K/month Enterprise deals early
- **Reality:** Takes 10x longer to close, same churn risk
- **Better:** Build 100 Pro subscribers in 6 months, then upsell 20% to Enterprise
- **Result:** Same revenue, lower CAC, faster growth

## Pricing FAQ

**Q: Why not $49/month to undercut everyone?**
A: Signals "cheap" not "affordable." $99 is the floor for serious B2B SaaS. Below that, you attract tire-kickers.

**Q: Why not $199/month to capture more value?**
A: Slows growth. At $99, we're an impulse purchase. At $199, we require budget approval. Speed > margin early on.

**Q: What if GovWin launches an AI newsletter for free?**
A: They won't. Their business model is selling seats, not newsletters. If they do, we're validated and can pivot to become their data source.

**Q: What about non-profit pricing?**
A: Not yet. Focus on paying customers first. Add non-profit discount (50% off) after we hit $50K MRR.

**Q: What about government agencies subscribing?**
A: Interesting. Not our ICP, but don't turn away. Charge Enterprise rate (government pays slowly but rarely churns).

**Q: Should we offer a free trial?**
A: No. Free tier IS the trial. Offering "14-day free trial of Pro" complicates onboarding and trains users to wait for deals.

**Q: What about annual contracts only?**
A: No. Monthly = lower friction = faster growth. Annual discount (2 months free) incentivizes committed buyers without forcing it.
