## Core Metrics Definitions

### CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
**Formula:** Total Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired

**Our CAC by Channel:**
- **Organic (LinkedIn, SEO, word-of-mouth):** $0 (time investment only)
- **Referral Program:** $50 (1 month free Pro = $99 cost / 2 referrals = $50 CAC)
- **Paid Ads (future):** $50-150 (estimated based on GovCon CPL benchmarks)

**Blended CAC Assumptions:**
- **Months 1-6:** $0 (100% organic)
- **Months 7-12:** $25 (90% organic, 10% paid/referral)
- **Year 2:** $75 (60% organic, 40% paid)

### LTV (Lifetime Value)
**Formula:** ARPU × Gross Margin × (1 / Monthly Churn Rate)

**Our LTV by Tier:**

**Pro Tier ($99/month, 5% churn, 98% margin):**
- Average customer lifetime: 20 months (1 / 0.05)
- LTV = $99 × 0.98 × 20 = **$1,940**

**Enterprise Tier ($499/month, 3% churn, 97% margin):**
- Average customer lifetime: 33 months (1 / 0.03)
- LTV = $499 × 0.97 × 33 = **$16,000**

**Blended LTV (70% Pro, 30% Enterprise):**
- ($1,940 × 0.70) + ($16,000 × 0.30) = **$6,158**

### LTV:CAC Ratio
**Formula:** LTV / CAC

**Our Ratios:**
- **Organic Growth:** $6,158 / $0 = ∞ (infinite, but not useful)
- **With Referral Program:** $6,158 / $50 = **123:1**
- **With Paid Ads (future):** $6,158 / $75 = **82:1**

**Benchmark:** 3:1 is the minimum for healthy SaaS. 5:1+ is excellent. We're at 80:1+.

### Payback Period
**Formula:** CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin)

**Our Payback Periods:**
- **Pro Tier:** $50 / ($99 × 0.98) = **0.5 months** (15 days)
- **Enterprise Tier:** $50 / ($499 × 0.97) = **0.1 months** (3 days)
- **Blended:** $50 / ($227 × 0.97) = **0.2 months** (6 days)

**Benchmark:** SaaS companies aim for <12 months. We're at <1 month.

### Gross Margin
**Formula:** (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue

**Our Gross Margins:**
- **Pro Tier:** ($99 - $2) / $99 = **98.0%**
- **Enterprise Tier:** ($499 - $15) / $499 = **97.0%**
- **Blended:** **97.5%**

**Benchmark:** SaaS companies target 70-80%. We're at 97%+.

### Magic Number (Sales Efficiency)
**Formula:** (Net New ARR in Quarter) / (Sales & Marketing Spend in Prior Quarter)

**Example (Q4 calculation):**
- Net New ARR (Q4): $450K (new subscriptions - churn)
- S&M Spend (Q3): $0 (organic)
- Magic Number: $450K / $0 = ∞

**With paid acquisition (Year 2):**
- Net New ARR (Q4): $300K
- S&M Spend (Q3): $50K
- Magic Number: $300K / $50K = **6.0**

**Benchmark:** >0.75 is efficient. 1.0+ is great. We're at 6.0.

## Unit Economics by Tier

### FREE TIER: Lead Generation Engine

**Purpose:** Build audience, prove value, convert to paid

**Economics:**
- **Revenue:** $0
- **Cost per Subscriber:** $0.10/month (email delivery)
- **Contribution Margin:** -$0.10 (loss leader)
- **Conversion Rate:** 5-10% to paid (4-8 week lag)
- **CAC Contribution:** Free tier reduces paid CAC by building trust

**Strategic Value:**
- 1,000 free subscribers = 50-100 paid subscribers (at 5-10% conversion)
- Free tier is the top of funnel (TOFU)
- Viral coefficient: 10% of free subscribers share with colleagues
- SEO benefit: More subscribers = more social shares = better organic reach

**Key Metric: Free-to-Paid Conversion Rate**
- Industry benchmark (newsletter): 3-8%
- Our target: 5-10% (high because we solve acute pain)
- Improvement drivers: onboarding emails, social proof, limited-time offers

## Sensitivity: What if Competitors Lower Prices?

**Scenario:** GovWin launches a $49/month AI newsletter to undercut us.

**Impact Analysis:**

**Option 1: Match Their Price ($49/month)**
- Pro tier LTV drops from $1,940 to $960
- LTV:CAC drops from 78:1 to 38:1 (still excellent)
- Need 2x subscribers to hit same MRR target

**Option 2: Hold Price, Differentiate on Quality**
- Keep $99/month pricing
- Compete on insight quality, not price
- Risk: Lose price-sensitive customers (estimated 30%)
- Benefit: Keep higher-value customers (70%)
- Net impact: -30% revenue short-term, but higher long-term LTV

**Option 3: Move Upmarket (Enterprise Focus)**
- De-emphasize Pro tier, focus on Enterprise
- Raise Enterprise price to $699/month
- Add more premium features (custom analysis, dedicated analyst)
- Target: Mid-size firms who can't afford GovWin's $35K/year

**Recommendation:** Option 2 (hold price, differentiate). We're not competing on price—we're competing on insight quality. Price-cutting race to the bottom is a losing strategy.

## Optimization Opportunities

### 1. Reduce Churn from 5% to 3%
**Impact:** LTV increases from $1,940 to $3,200 (+65%)
**How:**
- Improve onboarding (email drip campaign, video walkthroughs)
- Add "win tracking" feature (let users mark contracts they won after reading our intel)
- Monthly customer check-in emails (proactive support)
- Build community (Slack channel, monthly webinar)

### 2. Increase Free-to-Paid Conversion from 5% to 8%
**Impact:** 60% more paid subscribers with same free base
**How:**
- Better free tier onboarding (show value immediately)
- Social proof (testimonials, case studies)
- Scarcity (limited-time offer, first 100 subscribers get discount)
- Improve email CTAs (A/B test messaging)

### 3. Increase Pro-to-Enterprise Upsell from 15% to 25%
**Impact:** 67% more Enterprise revenue
**How:**
- Proactively offer team trials after 3 months on Pro
- Add "share this with your team" feature in Pro tier
- Email campaigns targeting firms with 5+ employees
- Offer "bring 2 teammates for $299" (cheaper than 3× Pro)

### 4. Improve Annual Contract Mix from 30% to 50%
**Impact:** Better cash flow, lower churn (annual customers churn less)
**How:**
- Increase annual discount from 17% to 25% (save 3 months)
- Offer annual-only bonuses (quarterly strategy calls)
- Default pricing page to annual (show monthly as alternative)

### 5. Add Upsell Features (Increase ARPU)
**Potential Add-Ons:**
- Custom NAICS alerts: +$20/month
- API access (for Pro tier): +$50/month
- Monthly strategy call: +$100/month
