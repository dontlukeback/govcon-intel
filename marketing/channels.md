## 2. Reddit Communities

### r/govcontracting (8,500+ members)
**Vibe:** Tactical, no BS, very active Q&A
**Best for:** Real talk from BD professionals, proposal managers, small business owners
**Content that works:**
- "I analyzed 500 contracts this week, here's what I found" (with data viz)
- "How to track recompetes without GovWin" (tactical how-to)
- AMAs (Ask Me Anything about federal contract intelligence)
**What doesn't work:** Direct sales pitches, generic content, anything that sounds like marketing
**Action items:**
- Post weekly summary of insights (not a sales pitch)
- Answer questions about contract tracking, NAICS codes, SAM.gov
- Offer to filter the report for specific users' NAICS codes (builds goodwill)

### r/smallbusiness (1.7M members)
**Vibe:** Broader audience, some GovCon content
**Best for:** Reaching small business owners exploring federal work
**Content that works:**
- "How small businesses can compete for federal contracts"
- "8(a) vs. SDVOSB: Which certification should you pursue?"
- Case studies of small firms winning federal work
**Action items:**
- Post once/month (not weekly — too frequent for this audience)
- Focus on educational content, not product pitches

- Free services: bid matching, proposal review, teaming

### Top PTACs to Target:

- Contact: coloradoptac.org
- Action: Sponsor their monthly newsletter ($200-$300)

**San Diego PTAC**

- Action: Partner on joint webinar series

**Alabama PTAC (Huntsville)**
- Army, missile defense contractors

## 10. Direct Outreach Channels

### Where to Find Target Customers:

**LinkedIn Sales Navigator** ($60/mo)
- Filter: Job titles (BD Director, Capture Manager, VP Business Development)
- Filter: Company size (20-500 employees)
- Filter: Location (DC Metro, Colorado Springs, San Diego, Huntsville)
- Filter: Industries (Defense, IT Services, Government Administration)
- Action: Send 20 personalized InMails/week with specific contract insight

**ZoomInfo** (Free trial, then $$$)
- Get verified email addresses for BD directors
- Action: Cold email 50 people/week with personalized recompete alert

**GovTribe** (Database of GovCon firms)
- Filter by NAICS codes, contract history, location
- Action: Export 100 firms, find their BD directors on LinkedIn, reach out

**USAspending.gov** (Free)
- Search for companies winning contracts in your target NAICS codes
- Action: Find them on LinkedIn, message: "Saw you won [contract name] at [agency]. We track recompetes in this space — here's what's coming up next."

## Red Flags: Channels to Avoid (for now)

1. **General sales/marketing groups** — wrong audience
2. **Overseas GovCon groups** — you're focused on U.S. federal
3. **Closed Facebook groups with <1,000 members** — too small
4. **Generic "entrepreneurs" or "startups" communities** — not specific enough
5. **Expensive conference booths ($5K+)** — not worth it until you have more traction

## Tracking & Metrics

### Per Channel:
- Signups attributed to each channel (use UTM codes)
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)
- Time spent vs. results

### Monthly Report:
- Which channels drove the most free signups?
- Which channels drove the most paid upgrades?
- Which channels have the best engagement rate (comments, replies, DMs)?

### Adjust based on data:
- If LinkedIn groups aren't converting → Try more direct outreach
- If newsletters aren't performing → Try guest posts instead
- If Reddit drives high engagement → Double down there
