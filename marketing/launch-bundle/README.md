## Troubleshooting (If Things Aren't Working)

### Problem: Low LinkedIn engagement (< 20 likes per post)
**Diagnosis:** Content isn't resonating or timing is off
**Fix:**
- Check posting time (9-10am EST Monday/Wednesday/Friday is optimal)
- Make first line more compelling (first 2 lines show in feed preview)
- Use more specific data (replace "contract value increased" with "$487M DISA contract")
- Add a visual (screenshot of report, data chart, etc.)

### Problem: Landing page visits but no signups
**Diagnosis:** Landing page copy or friction issue
**Fix:**
- Is the value prop clear in 5 seconds?
- Is "Free" prominent enough?
- Is the signup form simple (just email, not 10 fields)?
- Test removing "No credit card required" (might imply eventual cost)
- Add social proof ("52 GovCon BD teams already subscribed")

### Problem: Free signups but low email open rates
**Diagnosis:** Welcome email isn't compelling or landing in spam
**Fix:**
- Check spam score (use mail-tester.com)
- Improve subject line (test "Your first GovCon intel report" vs. "Welcome to GovCon Weekly Intelligence")
- Send from personal email (luke@domain.com) not no-reply@domain.com
- Make sure first email delivers VALUE immediately (don't just say welcome)

### Problem: Warm contacts aren't replying
**Diagnosis:** Email is too long or too salesy
**Fix:**
- Cut email length in half (5 sentences max)
- Lead with their interest, not your product
- Make it personal (reference something specific about them)
- Lower the ask (don't ask them to sign up, ask "Is this useful?")

### Problem: Week 1 ends with < 20 signups
**Diagnosis:** Need to increase top-of-funnel volume
**Fix:**
- Double LinkedIn outreach (40 connection requests instead of 20)
- Post in more GovCon groups (find 5 more on LinkedIn, Facebook, Slack)
- Write guest post for GovCon blog (ExecutiveBiz, WashingtonExec, GovConWire)
- Run $100 LinkedIn ad test (target "Business Development" + "Federal Government" + DMV area)
- Host a webinar ("3 Signals That Predict Recompete Wins") and promote it

## Questions? Problems? Pivots?

As you go through the launch, you'll hit unexpected issues. That's normal. Some quick guidance:

**If a LinkedIn post flops:** Don't delete it. Just move on to the next one. Analyze why later.

**If someone criticizes your product:** Respond thoughtfully, don't argue. Use it as feedback.

**If warm contacts ignore you:** Don't take it personally. Follow up once, then move on.

**If Reddit downvotes you:** Check if you were too promotional. Try again in 2 weeks with pure value post.

**If you get zero signups Day 1:** Don't panic. Signups accelerate over time as content compounds.

**If you want to quit after Day 3:** That's normal. Push through. Day 5-7 is when momentum builds.

**Remember: You're not selling snake oil. You're sharing real contract intel that helps small GovCons compete. The product is GOOD. Just get it in front of the right people.**

**Good luck. Go build something people want.**
