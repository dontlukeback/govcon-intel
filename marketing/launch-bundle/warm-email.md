# Warm Email Template (Personal Contacts)

**Target:** Warm contacts in your network (former colleagues, people you've met at conferences, LinkedIn connections you've chatted with)

**Goal:** Personal, casual introduction — "Hey, I built this thing, thought you might find it useful"

**Send Time:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 7-9am EST (catch them at desk with coffee)

---

## Template

**Subject:** Built something for [NAME] and the BD team at [COMPANY]

**Body:**
```
[NAME],

Hope you're doing well. Quick update from my end:

I just launched GovCon Weekly Intelligence — a curated newsletter that tracks federal IT contract intel (recompetes, trends, competitor moves). Think Bloomberg Brief but for small GovCon BD teams.

Knowing you and [COMPANY] work in [cybersecurity/cloud/AI/DevSecOps], figured this might be useful for your pipeline. Every Monday you get:

→ Recompete alerts 3-6 months before SAM.gov
→ Competitor tracking (who won what this week)
→ expert analysis with specific action items

First report is free, takes 8 minutes to read. If it's helpful, great. If not, no worries — just wanted to share.

[INSERT LANDING PAGE LINK]

How are things on your end? Still chasing [specific project/contract you know they were working on]?

— Luke

P.S. — If you know other BD folks who'd benefit from this, feel free to forward. Always looking for early feedback.
```

---

## Why This Works

1. **Personal opening** — "Hope you're doing well" = relationship-first, not sales-first
2. **Context on what you're doing** — Brief explanation without being salesy
3. **Personalized to their vertical** — Shows you remember what they work on
4. **Low pressure ask** — "If it's helpful, great. If not, no worries."
5. **Reciprocal question** — Asks about them, not just talking about you
6. **P.S. referral seed** — Plants the idea of forwarding without demanding it

---

## Personalization Checklist

Before sending to each contact, customize:

- [ ] **[NAME]** — Use first name only (more casual)
- [ ] **[COMPANY]** — Their actual company name
- [ ] **[vertical]** — Their specific focus area (cyber, cloud, AI, DevSecOps, data, etc.)
- [ ] **[specific project/contract]** — Reference something you know they're working on (from LinkedIn, past conversation, etc.)
- [ ] **Relationship context** — Add a line that proves you actually know them:
  - "Since we worked together at [Company]..."
  - "Ever since we met at [Conference]..."
  - "Remember when you mentioned you were struggling with [X]?"

## Follow-Up Strategy

### If they reply positively (e.g., "This looks great!" or "Signed up"):
```
Awesome! Let me know what you think after you read Friday's issue.

And if you have any feedback (features you want, verticals we should cover, etc.), I'm all ears. Building this based on what actual BD teams need.

Appreciate you checking it out.
```

### If they reply with a question (e.g., "How much does this cost?" or "How is this different from GovWin?"):
```
[Answer their question directly and thoroughly]

Want to see it in action? I can send you this week's full report right now (no signup required). Then you can decide if it's worth trying.

Sound good?
```

### If they don't reply after 5 days:
Send ONE follow-up, keep it light:

**Subject:** Re: Built something for [NAME] and the BD team at [COMPANY]

**Body:**
```
[NAME],

Circling back on this — I know inboxes get buried.

If you're interested, the free report link is still active: [link]

If not, no worries. Just wanted to make sure you saw it.

— Luke
```

**If they still don't reply:** Let it go. Don't be pushy with warm contacts.

## Metrics to Track

For warm email outreach, expect MUCH higher conversion than cold:

| Metric | Cold Email | Warm Email |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Open Rate** | 20-25% | 60-80% |
| **Reply Rate** | 5-8% | 25-40% |
| **Click-Through** | 10-15% | 40-60% |
| **Free Signup** | 2-4% | 20-35% |
| **Paid Conversion** | 1% | 10-15% |

**Why warm converts better:**
- Trust already established
- They know you're legit (not a scammer)
- They actually care about your success
- They're more likely to give honest feedback

## Bonus: Timing Strategy

**Best days to send warm emails:**
- **Tuesday** — People are past Monday chaos, not yet in midweek grind
- **Wednesday** — Peak productivity day, good response rates
- **Thursday** — Also good, people planning for next week

**Avoid:**
- **Monday** — Too much catch-up from weekend
- **Friday** — People checking out early

**Best times:**
- **7-9am EST** — Morning coffee, inbox zero attempt
- **12-1pm EST** — Lunch break email check
- **4-6pm EST** — End of day wrap-up

**Your warm contacts are your best early adopters. Treat them well.**
