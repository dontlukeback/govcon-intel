## Engagement Strategy for Each Post

### Do This:
1. **Tag 10 relevant people** in the post or comments (BD directors, capture managers you know)
2. **Respond to every comment** within 30 minutes (shows you're active, boosts algorithm)
3. **Reshare to your LinkedIn story** 4 hours after posting (second wave of impressions)
4. **Cross-post to Twitter/X** with slight modifications (remove hashtags, keep core message)
5. **Share in 3 GovCon groups** you're a member of (not as spam — as "thought this might be useful")

### Don't Do This:
1. Delete and repost if it doesn't get engagement immediately (gives algorithm negative signal)
2. Use generic AI-sounding language ("leverage", "synergy", "game-changing")
3. Over-hashtag (3-5 max, always end with #GovCon)
4. Ignore comments (kills engagement rate)
5. Post and ghost (need to be active in comments for 60-90 minutes after posting)

## Post Variations for A/B Testing

If a post doesn't perform well (< 50 impressions in first hour), try these variations:

### Variation 1: Lead with a question
Instead of: "I spent 10 years watching small GovCon firms miss recompetes"
Try: "How many recompetes did your BD team miss last quarter?"

### Variation 2: Lead with a stat
Instead of: "Small GovCon BD teams face an impossible choice"
Try: "73% of small GovCon firms miss recompetes because they can't afford intel tools."

### Variation 3: Lead with urgency
Instead of: "$487M contract at DISA expires in 104 days"
Try: "URGENT: $487M DISA contract expires in 104 days and the RFP still hasn't dropped."

### Variation 4: Shorter = better
If a post flops, cut it in half. LinkedIn algorithm favors posts that get read all the way through.

## Tracking & Optimization

### Metrics to track per post:
- Impressions (goal: 500+ in first 24 hours)
- Engagement rate (goal: 3%+)
- Comments (goal: 5+ per post)
- Link clicks (goal: 10+ per post)
- Follows from post (goal: 5+ per post)

### If a post underperforms:
1. Check timing (weekends = lower reach)
2. Check length (300+ words = people scroll past)
3. Check hook (first line has to stop the scroll)
4. Try variations above

### If a post overperforms:
1. Reshare it 7 days later with "In case you missed it"
2. Turn it into an email to subscribers
3. Use the same format for future posts
4. Make it a pinned post on your profile
