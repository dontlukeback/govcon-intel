# GovCon Weekly Intelligence — Launch Marketing Strategy

## Mission
Get 100 free subscribers in 30 days. Convert 10 to paid ($149/mo) within 60 days.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: Small/Mid GovCon BD Professionals
- **Titles**: BD Director, Capture Manager, VP of Business Development, Proposal Manager
- **Firm size**: $5M-$100M annual revenue, 20-500 employees
- **Pain**: Spending 10+ hours/week on manual SAM.gov searches, missing recompetes, can't afford GovWin/Deltek ($15K-$25K/yr)
- **NAICS codes**: 541512, 541519, 541511, 541330, 518210, 541715 (IT services, cyber, engineering)
- **Set-asides**: 8(a), SDVOSB, WOSB, HUBZone firms are PERFECT for this
- **Location**: DC Metro (Reston, Arlington, Tysons), Colorado Springs, San Diego, Huntsville

### Secondary: Solo BD Consultants & Small Business Owners
- Running BD for themselves or 2-5 person shops
- Can't justify enterprise tools but need competitive intel
- Will pay $149/mo if it saves them 5+ hours/week

### Tertiary: Federal Sales Teams at Tech Companies
- SaaS companies selling to federal (FedRAMP, IL4/IL5 products)
- Need to track which agencies are buying what, identify warm leads
- Corporate card budget, less price-sensitive

---

## Value Proposition

**For BD professionals at small/mid GovCon firms:**
"Stop missing recompetes and get competitor intel that costs $20K/yr elsewhere — for $149/mo."

**Proof points:**
- Real recompete alerts 3-6 months before SAM.gov posting
- Competitor tracking (who won what, option exercises, ceiling increases)
- AI analysis that tells you "so what" not just "what"
- 5-minute Monday morning read vs. 10 hours of manual research

## Launch Strategy: First 100 Subscribers in 30 Days

### Week 1: Seed + Validation (Days 1-7)
**Goal: 20 subscribers**

**Tactics:**
1. **Personal network blast** (Day 1)
   - Email 50 people in your network who work in GovCon
   - Subject: "I built something you'll actually use"
   - Body: 3 sentences + link to landing page + sample report
   - Ask: "Forward this to your BD team"

2. **LinkedIn founder content** (Days 1-7)
   - Post daily (see content calendar)
   - Share insight from the sample report with "This is why I built GovCon Weekly"
   - Tag 10 GovCon people per post (BD directors, capture managers)
   - Use hashtags: #GovCon #FederalContracting #BusinessDevelopment #SBIR #8a #SDVOSB

3. **Direct outreach to 20 target firms** (Days 1-7)
   - Find BD directors on LinkedIn
   - Send personalized message: "Saw you're at [Company]. We track [their NAICS codes]. Here's a recompete you might care about: [specific example from report]"
   - Not selling, just providing value

4. **GovCon subreddit + forums** (Days 3-5)
   - r/govcontracting — share sample report as "I analyzed 500 federal contracts this week, here's what I found"
   - GovTribe community forums
   - Don't spam, provide value

### Week 2: Community Expansion (Days 8-14)
**Goal: 50 total subscribers (+30 net new)**

**Tactics:**
1. **Guest post on GovCon blogs** (pitch Days 8-10, publish Days 12-14)
   - Target: GovCon Giant, Set-Aside Alert, WashingtonExec
   - Pitch: "5 Recompetes Your BD Team Should Be Tracking Right Now"
   - Include signup CTA at bottom

2. **LinkedIn newsletter launch** (Day 8)
   - Start "GovCon Intelligence Briefing" on LinkedIn
   - Publish weekly summary from the report
   - Promote on your personal profile

3. **Free webinar** (Day 14 @ 12pm EST)
   - Title: "How to Track Federal Recompetes Without GovWin"

   - Promote Days 8-13 on LinkedIn + email
   - Collect emails via Zoom registration

4. **Reddit AMA** (Day 10)
   - r/govcontracting: "I built an AI tool that tracks federal contracts. AMA."

## Language & Voice

**DO:**
- Use GovCon acronyms naturally (IDIQ, GWAC, OASIS, STARS, 8a, SDVOSB, PoP, BD, NAICS)
- Be direct and tactical ("Set a SAM.gov alert by Friday")
- Show you understand their pain ("10 hours/week on manual research")
- Use specific dollar amounts and timelines ("$12M recompete, 104 days to expiration")
- Sound like a peer, not a vendor ("Here's what I found this week")

**DON'T:**
- Use generic B2B SaaS language ("leverage", "synergy", "solutions")
- Over-explain acronyms (they know what NAICS means)
- Be salesy or hype-y ("revolutionary", "game-changing")
- Talk about features without outcomes (they don't care about AI, they care about catching recompetes)

**Tone:**
- Confident but not arrogant
- Tactical, not theoretical
- Insider, not outsider
- Data-driven, not hand-wavy

**Example sentences:**
- ✅ "Enterprise IT Support Services (EITSS-III) at DISA expires in 104 days. Leidos is the incumbent. Draft RFP drops in April. If you're targeting this, your capabilities brief is due by Friday."
- ❌ "Our AI-powered platform leverages machine learning to deliver actionable insights that transform your business development strategy."

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Low Conversion (Landing Page → Signup)
**Mitigation:**
- A/B test headlines ("Stop Missing Federal Contracts" vs. "Get Competitor Intel for $149, Not $20K")
- Add video explainer (2 min screen recording walking through sample report)
- Social proof: testimonials from early users
- Trust signals: "Built with USAspending.gov open data" + "No credit card required"

### Risk 2: Low Engagement (Free → Paid)
**Mitigation:**
- Welcome email sequence must deliver immediate value (not just "thanks for signing up")
- Offer personalized NAICS code filtering in Pro tier (show specific contracts they care about)
- Limited-time discount for early subscribers ("Founder's rate: $99/mo forever")

### Risk 3: Wrong Audience (Non-GovCon Signups)
**Mitigation:**
- Ask "What NAICS codes do you bid on?" in signup form
- Filter out non-.gov, non-.mil emails
- Send different onboarding for federal sales vs. GovCon firms

### Risk 4: Competitive Response (GovWin/Deltek Lower Prices)
**Mitigation:**
- Differentiate on simplicity, not just price ("5-minute read vs. 10-hour database dive")
- Target firms too small for enterprise tools
- Build community (Slack, LinkedIn) that enterprise tools don't provide
