Network Operations & Cyber Defense (NOCDef)

CYBERCOM | Incumbent: Booz Allen Hamilton | $312M | 119 days to expiration

Here's what makes this different: CYBERCOM is restructuring the entire contract into three task areas—Defensive Cyber Operations, Threat Intelligence Fusion, and Hunt Forward support. Multiple awards likely.

This is the best possible scenario for challengers. When an agency restructures a recompete, they're signaling dissatisfaction with the current approach. Booz Allen's incumbent advantage just got weaker.

Who can win this: You still need prime-tier credentials ($50M+ past performance), but the multiple-award structure means you don't have to beat Booz Allen on every task area. Pick your lane.

Action: Set a SAM.gov alert for NAICS 541519 + CYBERCOM. Start identifying teaming partners for complementary task areas. The Sources Sought is already published—you're late if you haven't responded yet.

Cybersecurity: Structural Shift, Not a Blip

NAICS 541512 is up +28% week-over-week. This isn't a blip—it's a structural shift driven by:

  • CMMC 2.0 driving assessment demand across the defense industrial base

Expect elevated spending through FY26 Q3. Agencies are front-loading cyber budgets before potential CR disruptions in Q4. If you're a cyber firm, your pipeline should be at maximum capacity right now.

AI/ML: From Pilots to Production

$1.3B in AI-related awards this week—and these aren't study contracts. They're production deployments. JADC2 at $1.24B. NGA training data at $78M. Scale AI just proved that AI firms can win real federal dollars, not just research grants.
