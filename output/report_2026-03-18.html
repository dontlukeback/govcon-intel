01 / DOGE Tracker
Federal Restructuring & Budget Impact
DOGE-driven workforce cuts at USAID and OPM are creating downstream contract disruptions -- 14 active contracts worth $2.3B have lost their COR or program office oversight this month alone.
● USAID: 80% RIF of acquisition workforce
127 active contracts ($4.1B) now have no assigned Contracting Officer's Representative. Stop-work orders issued on 23 task orders. Primes are burning fee on idle staff.
Status: Injunction filed -- temporary restraining order in effect through April 2, but agency is slow-walking re-staffing. Don't count on resumption.
● OPM: Consolidation into GSA proposed
HR IT modernization contracts ($380M across 6 vehicles) are in limbo. OPM issued informal 'continue performance' guidance but no funded modifications. Contractors billing at risk.
Status: Congressional review period -- expect 90-day delay minimum. If you're subbing on OPM work, get your prime's position in writing NOW.
● Department of Education: Agency downsizing / potential dissolution
FSA loan servicing contracts ($1.8B) are being reviewed for early termination. TIVAS contract holders (Maximus, Aidvantage) face T4C risk. Student loan tech modernization RFPs pulled from forecast.
Status: Legislation pending. No imminent action but pipeline is frozen. BD teams should pause capture spend on ED opportunities until Q3 FY26 clarity.
● EPA: 30% budget cut proposed for FY27
Superfund remediation contracts and environmental monitoring task orders likely to shrink. Current ceiling on EPA OLEM IDIQ ($420M) may not be fully obligated.
Status: Budget proposal stage -- Congress will negotiate, but signal is clear: environmental services declining 2+ years out.
● DoD / DISA: GROWING -- absorbing mission from other agencies
DISA picking up shared services IT work previously spread across civilian agencies. DES ceiling increase this week ($450M) is directly tied to this consolidation. Net positive for DISA-focused contractors.
Status: Active growth. Position here if you're losing civilian agency work.

07 / One to Watch
CJADC2 Tactical Edge AI -- The $3B Program Nobody's Talking About
DoD / Joint Staff J6 · Est. $3,000,000,000 - $5,000,000,000
Buried in the FY27 budget justification documents is a new program element for 'Combined Joint All-Domain Command and Control -- Tactical Edge Processing' (CJADC2-TEP). This is the Joint Staff's answer to the latency problem in JADC2: instead of backhauling all sensor data to cloud for AI processing, they want AI inference running on ruggedized edge devices at the battalion level and below. The program calls for federated learning across classification domains -- models that train on TS/SCI data but deploy inferences at the unclassified tactical edge.
Why It Matters: This is the next JEDI/JWCC-scale program, but for edge computing instead of cloud. The $1.24B GDIT award this week is the integration layer -- CJADC2-TEP is the compute layer underneath it. First RFI expected Q1 FY27. Estimated ceiling $3-5B over 10 years. The firms that shape this requirement in the next 6 months will own the competitive landscape for the next decade.
Start Positioning Now
‣ Read the FY27 PB justification book for PE 0604XXX (classified PE -- request through your cleared facility's SAPCO)
‣ Attend the Joint Staff J6 JADC2 Cross-Functional Team industry engagement on May 15 at the Pentagon (registration opens April 1)

‣ Identify your teaming strategy early: this will require hardware (ruggedized edge servers), software (ML inference frameworks), and integration (tactical network expertise). No single firm has all three.
‣ Connect with the GDIT JADC2 team -- they will be the prime integrator and will need edge computing subs. Their win this week makes them the center of gravity for this ecosystem.
