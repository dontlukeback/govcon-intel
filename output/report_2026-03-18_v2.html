Federal Procurement Health · March 2026

The federal procurement market is splitting into two speeds. DoD and IC are accelerating (cyber, AI, space), driving the spending velocity score up. If you're civilian-focused, the next 6-12 months will be turbulent. The set-aside activity uptick in Q3 is the one bright spot for small businesses on the civilian side.

Matter of Raytheon Intelligence & Space, B-422891
Raytheon Intelligence & Space v. Space Systems Command · Awardee: L3Harris Technologies · $890,000,000
SUSTAINED

Unequal evaluation of past performance. SSC rated L3Harris 'Outstanding' for a contract that was 14 months behind schedule, while rating Raytheon 'Good' for an on-time delivery. GAO found the evaluation was inconsistent and arbitrary.

BD Implication

SSC must re-evaluate past performance for both offerors. This likely delays the award by 90-120 days. L3Harris may still win on re-evaluation, but the pricing and technical scores are now exposed to scrutiny. If you lost on this competition, monitor for the corrective action announcement -- there may be an opportunity to submit a revised proposal.

Lesson: Document EVERYTHING about your on-time delivery performance. Agencies that rate incumbents favorably despite schedule slippage are vulnerable to protest. If you're better on schedule, make that the centerpiece of your past performance narrative.

Matter of Palantir Technologies, B-422956
Palantir Technologies v. Defense Intelligence Agency · Awardee: Databricks Federal · $124,000,000
DENIED

Palantir argued DIA's evaluation of technical approach was unreasonable because it favored open-source architectures over proprietary platforms. GAO found DIA's preference for 'vendor-agnostic, open-architecture solutions' was clearly stated in the solicitation and applied consistently.

BD Implication

The IC is serious about moving away from proprietary lock-in. DIA explicitly evaluated against vendor lock-in risk -- Palantir's Foundry platform was scored lower because of data portability concerns. This is a signal for ALL IC procurements: if your solution creates lock-in, you will be penalized.

Lesson: Build data portability and open standards into your technical approach. Agencies are no longer just saying they want open architecture -- they're scoring against it. If you're competing against Palantir or similar proprietary platforms, make vendor-agnosticism your #1 discriminator.

Matter of Deloitte Consulting, B-423001
Deloitte Consulting LLP v. Department of Veterans Affairs · Awardee: Accenture Federal Services · $389,000,000
DISMISSED (voluntary corrective action)

Deloitte protested the VA's cloud migration award to Accenture, arguing the FinOps evaluation factor was not adequately defined in the RFP. VA voluntarily took corrective action before GAO could rule -- they will amend the RFP to clarify the FinOps evaluation criteria and re-evaluate.

BD Implication

The $389M VA CMMS award we reported in this issue is now in corrective action. Accenture may still win, but the re-evaluation creates an opening for Deloitte and potentially other offerors. Watch for the amended solicitation -- the FinOps criteria will be more explicit, which may level the playing field.

Lesson: When an agency creates a new evaluation factor (like FinOps), push back during the Q&A period if it's vaguely defined. Deloitte's protest succeeded not because FinOps was wrong, but because the evaluation criteria for it were insufficiently defined. Vague criteria = protest opportunity.

CJADC2 Tactical Edge AI -- The $3B Program Nobody's Talking About

DoD / Joint Staff J6 · Est. Value: $3,000,000,000 - $5,000,000,000 · Timeline: RFI expected October-December 2026. Draft RFP late FY27. Award FY28.

Buried in the FY27 budget justification documents is a new program element for 'Combined Joint All-Domain Command and Control -- Tactical Edge Processing' (CJADC2-TEP). This is the Joint Staff's answer to the latency problem in JADC2: instead of backhauling all sensor data to cloud for AI processing, they want AI inference running on ruggedized edge devices at the battalion level and below. The program calls for federated learning across classification domains -- models that train on TS/SCI data but deploy inferences at the unclassified tactical edge.

Why It Matters

This is the next JEDI/JWCC-scale program, but for edge computing instead of cloud. The $1.24B GDIT award this week is the integration layer -- CJADC2-TEP is the compute layer underneath it. First RFI expected Q1 FY27. Estimated ceiling $3-5B over 10 years. The firms that shape this requirement in the next 6 months will own the competitive landscape for the next decade.

What to Do Now
  • Read the FY27 PB justification book for PE 0604XXX (classified PE -- request through your cleared facility's SAPCO)
  • Attend the Joint Staff J6 JADC2 Cross-Functional Team industry engagement on May 15 at the Pentagon (registration opens April 1)
  • If you have edge AI capabilities (NVIDIA Jetson, Qualcomm SNPE, or custom ASIC experience), start building a past performance portfolio with tactical use cases
  • Identify your teaming strategy early: this will require hardware (ruggedized edge servers), software (ML inference frameworks), and integration (tactical network expertise). No single firm has all three.
  • Connect with the GDIT JADC2 team -- they will be the prime integrator and will need edge computing subs. Their win this week makes them the center of gravity for this ecosystem.
