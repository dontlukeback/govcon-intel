DOGE WATCH

14 contracts lost their government oversight this month

DOGE workforce cuts are creating a problem nobody anticipated: contracts that are technically alive but have no Contracting Officer’s Representative watching them. USAID alone has 127 active contracts ($4.1B) with nobody on the government side. Stop-work orders hit 23 task orders.

The agency-by-agency breakdown:

  • USAID: 80% of acquisition workforce gone. TRO keeps contracts alive through April 2 but don’t count on resumption. If you’re a sub, invoke your T4C protections this week.
  • OPM: Being folded into GSA. $380M in HR IT contracts are unfunded. Get your prime’s position in writing.
  • Dept of Education: $1.8B in loan servicing under termination review. Pipeline frozen. Pause capture spend.
  • EPA: 30% FY27 budget cut proposed. Environmental services declining for 2+ years.
  • DISA: The exception — it’s growing. Absorbing civilian IT work. Reposition here if you’re losing civilian contracts.

Court watch: AFGE v. DOGE oral arguments March 28. If DOGE gets classified as a FACA advisory committee, all its recommendations become non-binding.

WHO WON THIS WEEK

$1.9B in new awards

GDIT takes $1.24B JADC2 contract from Army

Beat Leidos despite being 11% higher on price. The winning move: a “digital twin” testbed that cuts testing timelines by 14 months, plus Anduril as a sub for mesh networking. The Army paid more for speed and innovation. If you’re still proposing waterfall integration for C2 programs, you’re going to keep losing.

Accenture Federal wins $389M VA cloud migration

FinOps was the differentiator. They guaranteed measurable cloud cost reduction. VA got burned by lift-and-shift migrations that doubled hosting costs — they weren’t going to let that happen again. Deloitte’s AWS-only proposal and IBM’s hybrid pitch both lost. If your cloud practice doesn’t have FinOps practitioners, fix that before your next proposal.

Scale AI lands $78.5M NGA training data contract

First major NGA award to a pure-play AI company. NGA realized their bottleneck isn’t algorithms — it’s labeled training data at scale. Anthropic scored highest technically but lacked SCIF infrastructure. Takeaway: the IC is unbundling the AI value chain. Pick one layer and own it.

Raytheon takes $215M Space Ground Systems

Commercial-hybrid architecture won. AWS Ground Station for unclassified data, seamless handoff to classified. Cut infrastructure costs 35%. Space ground is going commercial-first — bespoke architectures are losing.

QUICK HITS

What else happened

  • SAIC DES gets another $450M ceiling bump — now $7.25B. Fourth increase in 18 months. DISA is eating everyone’s IT budget. If you’re not a DES sub, you’re locked out. SAIC hiring subs for cloud, IAM, endpoint security.
  • AWS IC cloud enters final option year ($187M). The follow-on will be multi-cloud, multi-award, $1.5–2B. Microsoft and Google both briefed the IC CIO. The real money is in the migration services layer ($200M+).
  • CIO-SP4 ceiling raised to $52B. NITAAC issued 47 new task orders in February alone (up from 28/mo avg). If you’re on it, staff a dedicated response team. Sweet spot: $5–25M health data analytics.
  • ManTech CMMC option exercised 45 days early. CMMC enforcement is accelerating. Start your Level 2 assessment now — C3PAO wait times are 4–6 months.
  • Peraton sole-sourced $128M FBI biometrics on Alliant 2. DOGE has flagged sole-source over $50M for review. Competitors: watch for a break in justification. Competitive follow-on would be $300M+.

ONE MORE THING

The $3–5B program buried in the FY27 budget docs

CJADC2-TEP — Tactical Edge Processing. The Joint Staff wants AI inference running on ruggedized edge devices at the battalion level instead of backhauling to the cloud. This is the next JEDI-scale program but for edge computing. First RFI expected Q1 FY27.

The $1.24B GDIT award this week is the integration layer. CJADC2-TEP is the compute layer underneath. If you have edge AI capabilities (NVIDIA Jetson, Qualcomm SNPE), start building your portfolio. And get to the Joint Staff J6 engagement on May 15 — registration opens April 1.
