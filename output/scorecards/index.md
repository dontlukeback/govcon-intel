## Key Findings

### Who's Winning

**General Dynamics IT** leads in volume with 44 awards and $2.7B total value. Their January-to-March trajectory shows consistent growth: $1.3M -> $1.6M -> $3.5M in monthly new awards, signaling steady pipeline execution. They dominate in Cloud and GSA vehicles.

**Booz Allen Hamilton** matches GDIT at $2.7B total but with fewer, larger contracts (avg $85.3M vs $62.2M). March showed a strong rebound to $36.8M after a quiet February ($793K), suggesting lumpy but high-value deal flow. Their Cloud vertical dominance reflects their analytics/AI positioning.

**Peraton** rounds out the $2.7B tier with the highest average deal size among volume players ($142.2M). Their February spike of $10.9M came from concentrated large awards. Fewer contracts but consistently massive deal sizes -- they are playing the big-ticket game.

### Who's Growing

**GDIT** is the clearest growth story in Q1 2026 -- three consecutive months of increasing award value. Their high volume (44 awards, most of any contractor tracked) suggests a broad pipeline across multiple agencies and vehicles.

**Booz Allen** shows a strong March recovery. The $36.8M in March awards after near-zero February activity suggests large deals closing, not a decline-and-recovery pattern. Their $2.7B total puts them firmly in the top tier.

### Who's Declining

**Deloitte** shows the clearest downward trend: $22.8M (Jan) -> $5.4M (Feb) -> $3.9M (Mar). While they have 29 awards total (tied with SAIC for volume in the mid-tier), the monthly trajectory is unmistakably negative. Their consulting-heavy model may be under pressure from agency cost-cutting.

**Leidos** and **SAIC** both had January activity but went quiet in February and March. For Leidos, this is notable given their $2.2B total portfolio -- their pipeline appears front-loaded. SAIC's $1.2B total with no recent awards suggests a similar pattern of lumpy procurement cycles rather than steady flow.

### Steady / Insufficient Data

**Accenture Federal Services** landed a massive $151.9M in February but has limited other Q1 activity. Their VA relationship (top agency) is a differentiator -- they are the only contractor in this list whose largest agency relationship is NOT GSA.

**ManTech** ($1.2B, 6 awards) and **Raytheon** ($421.1M, 2 awards) have too few awards in Q1 to establish a trend. ManTech's $202.1M average award size is the highest among contractors with 5+ awards, indicating they compete almost exclusively in large-scale programs.

**Scale AI** does not appear in the federal IT procurement data as a prime contractor. Their government AI/ML work likely flows through subcontracting arrangements or classified channels.

## Methodology

- **Data source:** 12,415 federal IT contract awards from 13 weekly collection cycles (Jan 1 - Mar 19, 2026)
- **Matching:** Fuzzy name matching on recipient_name (handles LLC/Inc/Corp variations and subsidiary names)
- **Value:** Total obligated award amounts; includes modifications to existing contracts
- **Trend:** Based on monthly award value trajectory in 2026 Q1 (Jan/Feb/Mar)
- **Verticals:** Classified by NAICS code and contract description analysis

*Generated by GovCon Weekly Intelligence. The more weeks of data we accumulate, the richer these scorecards become -- this is the data moat.*
