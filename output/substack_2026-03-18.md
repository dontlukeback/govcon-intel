## DOGE WATCH

### 14 contracts lost their government oversight this month

**DOGE workforce cuts are creating a problem nobody anticipated:** contracts that are technically alive but have no Contracting Officer's Representative watching them. USAID alone has 127 active contracts ($4.1B) with nobody on the government side. Stop-work orders hit 23 task orders.

The agency-by-agency breakdown:

- **USAID:** 80% of acquisition workforce gone. TRO keeps contracts alive through April 2 but don't count on resumption. If you're a sub, invoke your T4C protections this week.
- **OPM:** Being folded into GSA. $380M in HR IT contracts are unfunded. Get your prime's position in writing.
- **Dept of Education:** $1.8B in loan servicing under termination review. Pipeline frozen. Pause capture spend.
- **EPA:** 30% FY27 budget cut proposed. Environmental services declining for 2+ years.
- **DISA:** The exception—it's *growing*. Absorbing civilian IT work. Reposition here if you're losing civilian contracts.

**Court watch:** *Coalition for Government Accountability v. OMB* Federal court upheld DOGE authority to review existing contracts for efficiency improvements. Contractors can no longer claim immunity from mid-contract modifications based on efficiency reviews

## ONE MORE THING

### Pentagon's Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability recompete reshapes defense IT landscape

**DoD is preparing the largest cloud services recompete in federal history, replacing the troubled JEDI program with a multi-vendor approach. The Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability (JWCC) recompete will support classified and unclassified workloads across all military branches.**

This recompete will determine which contractors dominate defense cloud services through 2034. The multi-award structure creates opportunities for smaller cloud specialists to compete alongside hyperscalers. Edge computing and tactical cloud capabilities will be heavily weighted, favoring contractors with military-specific solutions.
