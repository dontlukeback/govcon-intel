## North Star Metric

**Paid Subscribers** — The single metric that best predicts long-term business success.

Why this metric?
- Validates willingness to pay (not just free attention)
- Correlates with revenue (ARPU × paid subscribers = MRR)
- Measures product-market fit (if people pay, we've solved a real problem)
- Drives all other metrics (acquisition, activation, retention, referral)

**Target trajectory:**
- Month 1: 50 paid subscribers ($4,950 MRR)
- Month 3: 200 paid subscribers ($19,800 MRR)
- Month 6: 500 paid subscribers ($49,500 MRR)
- Month 12: 1,000 paid subscribers ($99,000 MRR)

## Primary Metrics (Track Weekly)

### 1. Acquisition Metrics

#### Total Subscribers (Free + Paid)
**Definition:** Cumulative count of active email subscribers
**Target:** 500 by Week 4, 2,000 by Month 3, 10,000 by Month 12
**Measurement:** Beehiiv dashboard
**Why it matters:** Top of funnel. More subscribers = more potential paid conversions.

**Breakdown by source:**
- Landing page form submissions
- Referrals (forward to a friend)
- LinkedIn / social
- Direct outreach
- Partnerships (PTAC networks, industry groups)

**Red flags:**
- <100 subscribers in Week 1 (insufficient launch momentum)
- Week-over-week growth <10% (need better acquisition channels)
- Unsubscribe rate >5% (content not resonating)

#### Subscriber Growth Rate
**Definition:** (New subscribers this week / Total subscribers last week) × 100
**Target:** 15-20% week-over-week in Month 1, stabilizing to 5-10% by Month 3
**Measurement:** Week-over-week change in Beehiiv analytics
**Why it matters:** Measures momentum and product-market fit signal.

#### Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)
**Definition:** Total marketing spend / New subscribers acquired
**Target:** <$10 in Phase 1 (organic + low-cost channels), <$25 in Phase 2 (paid ads)
**Measurement:** Marketing spend / New subscriber count
**Why it matters:** Unit economics. If CPA > LTV, we're burning money.

**Breakdown by channel:**
- Organic (SEO, LinkedIn posts): $0-5 CPA
- Referrals: $0 CPA (viral growth)
- Direct outreach: $5-10 CPA (time cost)
- Paid ads (Phase 2+): $20-50 CPA

### 2. Activation Metrics

#### Email Open Rate
**Definition:** (Emails opened / Emails delivered) × 100
**Target:** >35% (industry benchmark for B2B = 30%)
**Measurement:** Beehiiv analytics
**Why it matters:** If users don't open, they don't see value. Low open rate = bad subject lines or wrong audience.

**Breakdown:**
- Free tier open rate (target: 30-35%)
- Paid tier open rate (target: 45-50%, paid users are more engaged)

**Tactics to improve:**
- A/B test subject lines (data-driven vs. curiosity-driven)
- Send time optimization (Monday 7 AM vs. Tuesday 9 AM)
- Personalization (first name in subject line)

#### Click-Through Rate (CTR)
**Definition:** (Unique clicks / Emails opened) × 100
**Target:** >12% (industry benchmark = 8-10%)
**Measurement:** Beehiiv link tracking
**Why it matters:** Measures engagement. If users open but don't click, content isn't actionable.

**Top clicked links to track:**
- USAspending.gov award links (validates interest in specific opportunities)
- SAM.gov alert setup instructions (measures action-taking)
- CSV export download (paid feature usage)
- "Upgrade to Pro" CTA (conversion intent signal)

**Red flags:**
- CTR <8% (content not engaging or too long)
- No clicks on CSV export (paid users not getting value)
- High clicks on unsubscribe link (content mismatch)

#### Time to First Value (TTFV)
**Definition:** Time from subscription to first "aha moment" (e.g., finding a relevant opportunity)
**Target:** <7 days (users should find value in their first newsletter)
**Measurement:** User survey + behavioral tracking (clicks on specific opportunities)
**Why it matters:** Users who get value fast are more likely to convert to paid and stick around.

**Aha moments:**
- Clicks on a recompete relevant to their company
- Downloads CSV export (paid users)
- Replies to newsletter with "This is exactly what I needed"
- Forwards newsletter to a colleague

### 3. Revenue Metrics

#### Paid Conversion Rate
**Definition:** (Paid subscribers / Total subscribers) × 100
**Target:** 10% by Month 1, 15% by Month 3, 20% by Month 6
**Measurement:** Stripe dashboard ÷ Beehiiv subscriber count
**Why it matters:** Core business model. If conversion <5%, pricing or value prop is wrong.

**Conversion funnel:**
1. Subscribe to free tier (100%)
2. Open first newsletter (60-70%)
3. Click on upgrade CTA (20-30%)
4. Visit pricing page (10-15%)
5. Start checkout (8-12%)
6. Complete purchase (10-12% of free base)

**Tactics to improve:**
- Free tier should show clear value but have obvious limitations (limited to top 10 opportunities)
- In-email upgrade prompts ("Want the full report? Upgrade to Pro")
- First-month discount for early adopters ("50% off your first month")
- Case studies from paid users ("How [Company] won a $4M contract with GovCon Intel")

#### Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
**Definition:** Total revenue from active paid subscriptions (month-over-month)
**Target:** $5K by Month 1, $20K by Month 3, $50K by Month 6, $100K by Month 12
**Measurement:** Stripe MRR dashboard
**Why it matters:** Primary business health metric. MRR growth = sustainable business.

**Breakdown:**
- New MRR (new subscriptions)
- Expansion MRR (upgrades from $99 → $149 or Team tier)
- Churned MRR (cancellations)
- Net New MRR = New + Expansion - Churned

#### Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)
**Definition:** Total MRR / Paid subscriber count
**Target:** $99 in Phase 1 (single tier), $120 in Phase 2 (mix of Pro and Team), $150 in Phase 3 (upsells)
**Measurement:** Stripe MRR ÷ Active paid subscribers
**Why it matters:** Measures pricing power and expansion potential.

**Improvement tactics:**
- Introduce Team tier ($499/mo for 5 users) in Phase 2
- Upsell features (API access, custom alerts, proposal assistance) in Phases 3-4
- Annual plans (2 months free = 16% ARPU increase)

#### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
**Definition:** (Sales + Marketing spend) / New paid customers acquired
**Target:** <$200 in Phase 1 (organic growth), <$500 in Phase 2 (paid ads)
**Measurement:** Total spend ÷ New paid subscriber count
**Why it matters:** Unit economics. Must be <⅓ of LTV for healthy business.

**Breakdown by channel:**
- Organic (content, SEO): $50-100 CAC
- Referrals: $0-50 CAC
- Direct outreach: $100-200 CAC
- Paid ads (Phase 2): $300-500 CAC

### 4. Retention Metrics

#### Monthly Churn Rate
**Definition:** (Paid subscribers who canceled this month / Total paid subscribers at start of month) × 100
**Target:** <5% monthly (industry benchmark for B2B SaaS = 3-7%)
**Measurement:** Stripe churn report
**Why it matters:** If churn >10%, we have a product problem. Users aren't getting value.

**Acceptable churn reasons:**
- Budget cuts (company downsizing)
- Role change (user left BD function)
- Seasonal (government shutdown, end of fiscal year slowdown)

**Red flag churn reasons:**
- "Not finding relevant opportunities" (targeting problem)
- "Too expensive for the value" (pricing or value prop issue)
- "Newsletter too long / too much info" (content/format problem)
- "Already have GovWin / Bloomberg Gov" (differentiation problem)

**Tactics to reduce churn:**
- Onboarding sequence (Week 1: "How to get the most value from GovCon Intel")
- Usage monitoring (alert sales team if user hasn't opened newsletter in 2 weeks)
- Win-back campaigns (offer 50% discount for 3 months to churned users)
- Exit surveys (understand why they left, improve product)

#### Retention Cohort Analysis
**Definition:** % of users from a given signup month who are still active N months later
**Target:**
- Month 1 retention: 90% (most users stay at least one billing cycle)
- Month 3 retention: 75% (users who make it to 3 months are sticky)
- Month 6 retention: 60% (long-term users)
- Month 12 retention: 50% (annual cohort baseline)

**Measurement:** Cohort table in Stripe or build custom dashboard
**Why it matters:** Shows product stickiness over time. Retention curves that flatten = product-market fit.

#### Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
**Definition:** ARPU ÷ Monthly churn rate
**Target:** $1,980 (assuming $99 ARPU and 5% churn = 20 months average lifetime)
**Measurement:** ARPU × Average customer lifetime (months)
**Why it matters:** Unit economics. LTV must be >3× CAC for sustainable business.

**Example calculation:**
- ARPU = $99/month
- Monthly churn = 5%
- Average lifetime = 1 ÷ 0.05 = 20 months
- LTV = $99 × 20 = $1,980

**Improvement tactics:**
- Reduce churn (every 1% churn reduction = 10% LTV increase)
- Increase ARPU (upsells, annual plans)
- Extend lifetime (better onboarding, community building)

#### Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
**Definition:** ((Starting MRR + Expansion MRR - Churned MRR - Contraction MRR) ÷ Starting MRR) × 100
**Target:** >100% by Month 6 (expansion revenue exceeds churn)
**Measurement:** Monthly MRR reconciliation
**Why it matters:** Best predictor of long-term growth. NRR >100% = product grows without new customers.

**Phase 1 reality:** NRR will be <100% (single $99 tier, no expansion). This is fine for MVP.
**Phase 2+ goal:** NRR >100% with Team tier, API access, and feature upsells.

### 5. Engagement Metrics

#### Newsletter Read Rate (Scroll Depth)
**Definition:** % of users who scroll to the end of the email
**Target:** >40% (users who read full report are most engaged)
**Measurement:** Email analytics (if available in Beehiiv) or user survey
**Why it matters:** Long-form content is risky. If users don't read past the executive summary, we should shorten.

**Breakdown by section:**
- Executive Summary: 90-100% (everyone reads)
- Recompete Analysis: 60-70% (core value prop)
- Agency Trends: 40-50% (secondary interest)
- Vertical Deep Dive: 30-40% (niche interest)
- Quick Hits: 50-60% (fast scan)

**Tactics to improve:**
- Shorten newsletter (4,000 → 3,000 words)
- Break into sections with clear headers (scannable)
- Prioritize most valuable content first (recompetes at top)
- Offer "Web version" link for users who prefer reading in browser

#### Feature Usage (Paid Tier)
**Definition:** % of paid users who use specific features
**Targets:**
- CSV export: >50% (if <30%, feature isn't discoverable or valuable)
- Click on USAspending links: >80% (validates core use case)
- Reply to newsletter: 10-15% (engagement signal)
- Forward to colleague: 20-30% (virality signal)

**Measurement:** Beehiiv link tracking + Stripe customer tags
**Why it matters:** Identifies which features drive retention vs. which are unused (candidates for removal).

#### Referral Rate
**Definition:** (New subscribers from referrals / Total subscribers) × 100
**Target:** 15-20% by Month 3 (viral growth)
**Measurement:** Beehiiv referral tracking
**Why it matters:** Organic growth lever. High referral rate = product is valuable enough to share.

**Tactics to increase:**
- Referral incentive program (give 1 month, get 1 month free)
- "Forward to a friend" CTA in every newsletter
- Social sharing buttons (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Case studies highlighting users who share with their teams

#### Net Promoter Score (NPS)
**Definition:** "How likely are you to recommend GovCon Intel to a colleague?" (0-10 scale)
**Target:** >40 by Month 3, >60 by Month 12 (industry benchmark for B2B SaaS = 30-50)
**Measurement:** In-app survey (email or landing page)
**Why it matters:** Best proxy for product love. NPS >50 = users are evangelists.

**Segments:**
- Promoters (9-10): Our evangelists. Ask for testimonials, case studies, referrals.
- Passives (7-8): Satisfied but not excited. Upsell or improve product.
- Detractors (0-6): At-risk churners. Immediate outreach to understand problems.

**Survey cadence:** Month 1, Month 3, Month 6, then quarterly.

### 6. Content Quality Metrics

#### Data Freshness
**Definition:** Time from award publication (USAspending) to newsletter delivery
**Target:** <7 days (awards from March 16-22 → newsletter on March 23)
**Measurement:** Manual tracking (award date vs. newsletter send date)
**Why it matters:** Timeliness is competitive advantage. Stale data = users find opportunities elsewhere.

**Phases:**
- Phase 1: Weekly (7-day lag is acceptable)
- Phase 2: Daily alerts (2-hour lag target)
- Phase 3+: Real-time (instant notifications)

#### Insight Accuracy
**Definition:** % of recompete predictions that are correct (e.g., "Incumbent vulnerable" → challenger actually wins)
**Target:** >70% by Month 6 (requires historical tracking)
**Measurement:** Manual follow-up on recompetes we analyzed (6-12 months later)
**Why it matters:** Trust. If our predictions are wrong, users stop relying on us.

**Validation process:**
- Track outcomes for every recompete we analyze
- Label predictions (STRONG incumbent advantage → Did incumbent win?)
- Refine AI prompts based on patterns (where did we get it wrong?)

#### User-Reported Value
**Definition:** % of users who say "I found a relevant opportunity in this week's newsletter"
**Target:** >60% by Month 3
**Measurement:** In-newsletter poll or user survey
**Why it matters:** Core value prop. If users aren't finding opportunities, we're not solving the problem.

**Survey question:** "Did you find at least one relevant opportunity in this week's newsletter?" (Yes/No)

## Experimentation Framework

### A/B Test Calendar (Month 1)

**Week 1: Subject Line Test**
- Control: "GovCon Intel: $890M in recompetes hitting in 60 days"
- Variant: "3 recompetes you need to track this week"
- Metric: Open rate
- Sample size: 100 users per variant
- Decision: Use winner for Week 2

**Week 2: CTA Test**
- Control: "Upgrade to Pro" button at top of email
- Variant: "Upgrade to Pro" button at bottom after recompete section
- Metric: Click-through rate on upgrade CTA
- Sample size: 200 users per variant
- Decision: Use winner for Week 3

**Week 3: Pricing Test**
- Control: $99/month
- Variant: $79/month (first month only)
- Metric: Paid conversion rate
- Sample size: 300 users per variant
- Decision: Determine optimal introductory pricing

**Week 4: Content Length Test**
- Control: 4,000-word newsletter
- Variant: 2,500-word newsletter (shortened)
- Metric: Read rate (scroll depth), CTR
- Sample size: 400 users per variant
- Decision: Optimize newsletter length for engagement

1. **10% paid conversion is achievable** → Test in first 4 weeks

## Success Definition (12-Month Horizon)

**Minimum viable outcome:**
- 1,000 paid subscribers
- $100K MRR
- <5% monthly churn
- NPS >50
- Profitable unit economics (LTV:CAC >3:1)

**Stretch outcome:**
- 2,500 paid subscribers
- $250K MRR
- <3% monthly churn
- NPS >60
- First enterprise customer ($50K+ annual contract)
- Product expands beyond newsletter (daily alerts, opportunity matching)

**Failure criteria (re-evaluate strategy):**
- <500 paid subscribers by Month 12
- Churn >10%
- NPS <30
- LTV:CAC <1:1 (burning money on every customer)
