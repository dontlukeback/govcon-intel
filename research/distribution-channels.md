**Status:** 0 subscribers, seeking FREE channels to launch TODAY

## Executive Summary

The GovCon BD community is fragmented across industry trade publications, professional associations, and niche platforms. There is NO dominant online community (no active Reddit, limited Slack/Discord presence detected). The best distribution opportunities are:

1. **Guest contributions** to established GovCon media outlets (GovCon Wire, ExecutiveBiz, FedScoop)
2. **LinkedIn content** targeting GovCon professionals and groups
3. **Professional association newsletters** (AFCEA, PSC, NDIA)
4. **PTAC/Apex Accelerator partnerships** for direct small business access
5. **Industry event platforms** (GovEvents) for visibility

## Key Insights

1. **No dominant online community** - Unlike tech (Reddit/HN) or marketing (Twitter), GovCon is fragmented
2. **Trade publications are gatekeepers** - GovCon Wire, ExecutiveBiz, ExecutiveGov are the "front page of GovCon"
3. **LinkedIn is the social hub** - Professional discussion happens on LinkedIn, not Reddit/Twitter
4. **Associations are valuable but expensive** - AFCEA, PSC, NDIA have reach but require membership
5. **PTACs are the hidden gem** - Direct access to small businesses actively pursuing GovCon, and they WANT resources to share
6. **Events are relationship-based** - Speaking opportunities exist but require networking and credibility
7. **Content quality matters** - GovCon audience is professional, expects substance over fluff
