# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
## GovCon Weekly Intelligence

**Last Updated:** March 18, 2026

## Primary ICP: Small/Mid GovCon IT Firms

### Firmographic Criteria

| Attribute | Target Profile |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Revenue Range** | $5M - $100M annual federal revenue |
| **Employee Count** | 20-500 employees |
| **Primary Industry** | IT Services, Cybersecurity, Cloud, Software Development |
| **Contract Types** | Federal prime contracts, subcontracts on major vehicles |
| **Geographic Focus** | National, with particular density in DMV, Colorado Springs, San Diego |
| **Certifications** | 8(a), SDVOSB, HUBZone, WOSB, SDB (at least one) |
| **NAICS Codes** | 541511, 541512, 541513, 541519, 541690, 518210, 541330 |

### Behavioral Indicators

**Must Have:**
- Active pursuit of federal contracts (10+ bids/year)
- Presence on USAspending.gov with recent awards
- SAM.gov active registration
- Track record of winning contracts in our 9 verticals (AI/ML, Cybersecurity, Cloud, Data Analytics, DevSecOps, Zero Trust, FedRAMP, Identity Management, Networking/SDWAN)

**Nice to Have:**
- Past performance on GSA Schedules, GWAC vehicles (CIO-SP3, OASIS, Alliant)
- Growing pipeline (YoY revenue growth 10%+)
- Recent contract wins showing momentum
- Job postings for BD/capture roles

### Psychographic Profile

**Pain Points:**
1. **Intelligence Gap**: Can't afford GovWin ($20K/year), GovTribe ($10K+), Bloomberg Government ($8K+)
2. **Resource Constraints**: 1-3 person BD teams trying to cover entire federal landscape
3. **Opportunity Overload**: Don't know which RFPs to chase vs. ignore
4. **Recompete Timing**: Miss critical recompete windows on contracts they could win
5. **Trend Blindness**: Don't see emerging opportunities in their verticals until it's too late
6. **Data Fragmentation**: Manually checking USAspending, SAM.gov, FedBizOpps across agencies

**Values:**
- Scrappy, resourceful operators
- Value actionable intelligence over raw data dumps
- Prefer "tell me what matters and why" over "here's 10,000 records"
- Willing to pay for tools that save BD time and increase win rates
- Trust recommendations from other small GovCon owners

**Decision-Making:**
- BD Director or VP Business Development (day-to-day user)
- CEO/President signs off on subscription ($50-200/month range)
- 2-4 week sales cycle for paid conversion
- Trial period critical (need to see value in 2-3 issues)

## Secondary ICP: Emerging GovCon Firms

### Firmographic Criteria

| Attribute | Target Profile |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Revenue Range** | $500K - $5M annual federal revenue |
| **Employee Count** | 5-50 employees |
| **Stage** | Post-first-contract, scaling phase |
| **Primary Industry** | IT Services, Consulting, Professional Services |
| **Certifications** | Recently certified 8(a) or SDVOSB (within 3 years) |

### Behavioral Indicators

**Must Have:**
- At least 1 federal contract win in past 24 months
- Active SAM.gov registration
- Evidence of BD capability (website mentions federal work)

**Nice to Have:**
- Graduated from accelerators (Bunker Labs, VetsinTech, Patriot Boot Camp)
- Active in NDIA, PSC, ACT-IAC, chamber of commerce events
- LinkedIn presence showing federal focus

### Psychographic Profile

**Pain Points:**
1. **Learning Curve**: Don't know what they don't know about federal procurement
2. **First BD Hire**: Considering hiring BD but need deal flow to justify it
3. **Agency Uncertainty**: Don't know which agencies align with their capabilities
4. **Prime vs. Sub**: Unclear when to bid prime vs. pursue subcontracting

**Values:**
- Education-first approach (need context, not just data)
- Community-oriented (value peer learning)
- Cost-conscious but willing to invest in growth tools
- Looking for "unfair advantages" to compete against established players

**Decision-Making:**
- CEO/Founder is often the BD person
- Fast decision cycle (same day to 1 week)
- Price sensitive ($50-100/month max initially)
- Need quick wins to justify continued subscription

## Tertiary ICP: Adjacent Service Providers

### Target Segments

**1. Proposal Writing Firms**
- Serve small GovCon clients
- Use our intelligence to inform capture strategies for clients
- Potential reseller/referral partners

**2. GovCon Consultants/Advisors**
- BD consultants serving multiple small firms
- SBA mentor-protégé program mentors
- Potential for multi-seat enterprise deals

**3. PTAC (Procurement Technical Assistance Centers)**
- Government-funded small business assistance orgs
- Potential partnership for bulk subscriptions to support constituents

## Anti-ICP: Who We Don't Target

### Wrong Size
- **Too Large**: Primes with $500M+ revenue (already have GovWin)
- **Too Small**: Pre-revenue, no SAM.gov registration, no past performance

### Wrong Sector
- **Construction/Facilities**: Not in our 9 tech verticals
- **Manufacturing/Supply**: Product-focused vs. services
- **R&D/SBIR**: Grant-focused vs. contract-focused

### Wrong Behavior
- **Passive Federal**: Government is <20% of revenue, not strategic
- **Subcontract-Only**: Never bid prime, don't need opportunity intelligence
- **Single Agency**: Sole focus on one agency (e.g., VA-only), already have insider knowledge

## ICP Scoring Model

Use this scoring framework to qualify prospects (0-100 scale):

| Criteria | Points | How to Score |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| **Revenue in Range** ($5M-$100M) | 25 | Check USAspending total over 12 months |
| **Recent Contract Wins** (past 6 months) | 20 | Awards in our 9 verticals |
| **Certified Small Business** (8a/SDVOSB/HUBZone) | 15 | Verify on SAM.gov or company website |
| **Multiple Agencies** (3+ agencies) | 15 | Diversification = need intelligence |
| **Active BD Team** (2+ BD people on LinkedIn) | 10 | Job titles: BD, Capture, Proposal |
| **Website Quality** (professional, updated) | 5 | Indicates maturity |
| **Content Engagement** (newsletter opens, clicks) | 5 | If existing free subscriber |
| **LinkedIn Presence** (company + exec activity) | 5 | Reachability signal |

**Qualification Tiers:**
- **80-100 pts**: Hot prospect - immediate outreach, personalized approach
- **60-79 pts**: Warm prospect - standard outreach sequence
- **40-59 pts**: Cold prospect - email nurture, wait for trigger event
- **<40 pts**: Not qualified - do not pursue

## Success Metrics for ICP Fit

**Leading Indicators (within 30 days of subscription):**
- Opens 3+ consecutive newsletters
- Clicks through to 5+ opportunities
- Responds to "what are you seeing?" feedback email
- Refers a peer firm

**Lagging Indicators (within 90 days):**
- Renews paid subscription after trial
- Upgrades to higher tier (if applicable)
- Provides testimonial or case study
- Expands to multiple seats

## ICP Refinement Cadence

**Monthly:**
- Review conversion rates by ICP segment
- Analyze churn reasons (wrong fit vs. product gap)
- Update qualification scoring based on patterns

**Quarterly:**
- Refresh revenue ranges based on market data
- Adjust vertical focus based on federal spending trends
- Add/remove certification types based on performance

**Annually:**
- Major ICP overhaul if needed
- Consider new adjacent segments
- Retire underperforming segments
