# Sales Playbook: Free to Paid Conversion
## GovCon Weekly Intelligence

**Last Updated:** March 18, 2026

---

## Overview

This playbook defines how we convert free newsletter subscribers into paying customers. Our conversion model is based on **value demonstration** over time, not aggressive sales tactics. GovCon BD professionals are sophisticated buyers — they'll pay when they see ROI, not because we pressure them.

**Core Philosophy:** Let the product do the selling. Our job is to make sure they USE the product and understand the value.

---

## Conversion Funnel

```
Free Subscriber
  ↓ (Week 1-2: Activation)
Engaged Free Subscriber
  ↓ (Week 3-4: Value Recognition)
High-Intent Prospect
  ↓ (Week 4-6: Conversion Trigger)
Paid Subscriber
  ↓ (Month 2+: Expansion)
Power User / Champion
```

---

## Stage 1: Activation (Week 1-2)
**Goal:** Get them to open, read, and click through on at least 2 of the first 3 issues

### Success Metrics
- Opens 2+ issues (within first 3 sent)
- Clicks through to 3+ opportunities
- Spends 4+ minutes reading (tracked via email analytics)
- Does NOT unsubscribe

### Tactics

If they don't open 2+ of the first 3 issues, move them to a low-touch nurture sequence:

- Goal: keep them warm for future re-engagement

### Objection Handling (Sales Calls)

#### Objection 1: "It's too expensive for a newsletter."

**Response:**
"I totally get it — $149/month feels like a lot for email.

But here's the thing: this isn't just a newsletter. It's a BD intelligence platform.

Think about it:
- Your BD Director makes ~$80K/year = $40/hour
- Manually tracking competitors + recompetes = 5 hours/week = $200/week = $800/month in labor
- Pro tier costs $149/month and does that work for you

So you're actually SAVING $650/month in BD time.

Plus, if this intel helps you close one extra $100K contract per year, it's paid for itself 8x over.

Does that math make sense?"

---

#### Objection 2: "We don't have budget right now."

**Response:**
"Understood. Most BD teams are tight on budget.

Two options:

**Option 1:** Stay on the free tier for now. It's still valuable. When budget opens up (Q3? Next fiscal year?), we'll revisit.

**Option 2:** If you can justify it as a business development cost (not a software subscription), it might come from a different bucket. Have you checked with your CFO?

Either way, no pressure. The free tier isn't going anywhere."

---

#### Objection 3: "I need to see more ROI first."

**Response:**
"Totally fair. What would you need to see to feel confident in the ROI?

Is it:
- A case study from another firm showing results?
- Tracking your own usage and results for another month?
- A specific feature (like competitor tracking) that would make it a no-brainer?

Let's figure out what 'proof of value' looks like for you, and then revisit. I'm not interested in selling you something that won't pay for itself."

---

#### Objection 4: "We already use [GovWin / Bloomberg Gov / etc.]."

**Response:**
"That's great — those are powerful tools. We're not a replacement, we're a complement.

GovWin gives you the universe of opportunities. We give you the curated shortlist for YOUR business.

Most of our Pro subscribers use both:
- GovWin for deep dives on specific opportunities
- GovCon Weekly Intelligence for weekly signal + competitor tracking

Think of it like this: GovWin is Google. We're your personal librarian who knows exactly what you're looking for.

Want to try Pro for 30 days and see if it adds value on top of GovWin? No risk."
