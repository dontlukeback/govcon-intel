# GovCon Weekly Intelligence — Q1 2026 OKRs
**Quarter:** Q1 2026 (March 18 - June 18, 2026) — 90 days
**Company Stage:** Pre-launch → Early traction
**Strategic Theme:** Prove the wedge. Validate that GovCon BD teams will consume weekly intelligence.

---

## Company Mission (North Star)

Become the Bloomberg Terminal for small/mid government contractors — starting with a weekly intelligence newsletter that tells BD teams exactly which opportunities to chase and why.

**This quarter's focus:** Prove people want this. Everything else is a distraction.

---

## Q1 Objectives & Key Results

### Objective 1: Launch and Validate Product-Market Fit
*"Prove that GovCon BD professionals will consume our intelligence and find it actionable."*

**Key Results:**
1. **500 email subscribers** by June 18 (currently 0)
   - Measure: Beehiiv subscriber count
   - Milestone: 100 by March 25, 250 by April 30, 500 by June 18

2. **40%+ average open rate** across 12 newsletters sent
   - Measure: Beehiiv analytics
   - Industry benchmark: 25-30% for B2B newsletters
   - This proves the content is relevant and valuable

3. **10+ unsolicited testimonials** from subscribers ("this saved me X hours" or "we pursued a contract because of your intel")
   - Measure: Email replies, LinkedIn comments, survey responses
   - This proves the intel is actionable, not just interesting

4. **3+ subscribers convert to paid tier** (once pricing is set)
   - Measure: Stripe/Beehiiv payment records
   - This proves willingness to pay, even at early stage

---

### Objective 2: Build a Reliable Weekly Intelligence Pipeline
*"Automate the data → insights → newsletter flow so it runs without manual intervention."*

**Key Results:**
1. **12 consecutive newsletters published** (every Monday, March 24 - June 16)
   - Measure: Beehiiv send logs
   - Zero missed weeks. Reliability = trust.

2. **Generate newsletter in <2 hours** of manual effort per week
   - Measure: Time tracking log
   - Milestone: 4 hours (March), 3 hours (April), 2 hours (May-June)
   - Automation goal: 90% of report generation is scripted by June

3. **Zero data quality errors** reported by subscribers
   - Measure: Error reports via email/feedback form
   - Track: contract values, agency names, dates, NAICS codes

4. **Add 2 new data sources** beyond USAspending API
   - Options: SAM.gov entity API, GovWin, Deltek, FPDS contract actions
   - This deepens the moat and improves intel quality

---

### Objective 3: Establish Repeatable Growth Channels
*"Identify the 2-3 channels that reliably bring in subscribers, then double down."*

**Key Results:**
1. **3 acquisition channels tested** with >20 subscribers each
   - Test: LinkedIn organic, direct outreach, GovCon communities, partnerships, SEO
   - Measure: UTM tracking + Beehiiv referral source

2. **1 channel scaled to 50+ subscribers/month** by June
   - Identify the winner, then systemize it
   - Example: If LinkedIn works, post 3x/week with consistent format

3. **Referral rate of 10%** (10% of subscribers share with a colleague)
   - Measure: Beehiiv referral program tracking
   - Incentive: "Refer 3 people, get early access to Pro tier"

4. **SEO foundation in place**: 10 blog posts published targeting GovCon keywords
   - Examples: "How to track federal recompetes," "DoD contracting vehicles explained"
   - Measure: Google Search Console impressions + clicks
   - Goal: Start ranking for long-tail GovCon search queries

---

### Objective 4: Define and Validate Business Model
*"Figure out who pays, how much, and for what — then test it."*

**Key Results:**
1. **Pricing model finalized** by April 15
   - Free tier: Weekly newsletter (what we have now)
   - Pro tier: Daily alerts, advanced filters, API access, custom reports
   - Enterprise tier: White-label, analyst support, custom integrations
   - Pricing hypothesis: $99/mo (Pro), $999/mo (Enterprise)

2. **10 pre-sales conversations** with potential Pro/Enterprise customers
   - Measure: Calendar invites + notes log
   - Goal: Understand willingness to pay + feature priorities

3. **$5,000 in revenue** by June 18 (even if pre-orders or pilot contracts)
   - Measure: Stripe/bank deposits
   - This proves someone will pay real money for this

4. **1 enterprise pilot contract signed** (3-month pilot with a GovCon firm)
   - Value: $2,500-5,000
   - Deliverable: Custom weekly intel brief for their BD team

## Anti-Goals (What We're NOT Doing This Quarter)

**Do not:**
- Build a SaaS dashboard or web app (newsletter is the wedge)
- Raise funding (bootstrap as long as possible)
- Hire employees (solo + AI for now)
- Expand beyond 9 verticals (focus > breadth)
- Chase every feature request (ruthless prioritization)
- Spend money on paid ads (organic + direct outreach only)

**Why:** Limited time and attention. We're validating demand, not scaling prematurely.

## Success Criteria for Q1

At the end of 90 days, we should be able to answer "yes" to these questions:

1. **Do people want this?**
   - 500+ subscribers, 40%+ open rate, 10+ testimonials = YES

2. **Will they pay for it?**
   - 3+ paid conversions, 1 enterprise pilot = YES

3. **Can we deliver it reliably?**
   - 12 consecutive newsletters, <2 hours/week effort = YES

4. **Is there a moat?**
   - Proprietary insights, automation, data sources competitors don't have = YES

If we can answer "yes" to all four, we have a business. If not, we pivot or kill it.

## Final Word

OKRs are not a to-do list. They're a forcing function to stay focused on what matters.

**This quarter, only 4 things matter:**
1. Can we get 500 people to subscribe?
2. Can we deliver weekly intel that's actually useful?
3. Can we do it without burning out?
4. Can we prove someone will pay for this?

Everything else is a distraction.

If you're working on something not tied to these 4 questions, stop and ask: "Why am I doing this?"
