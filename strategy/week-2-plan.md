## Experiment Design

### Experiment 1: Landing Page vs. Sample Report (A/B Test)
**Hypothesis:** People convert better when they see the product first (sample report) vs. reading marketing copy (landing page).

**Setup:**
- Group A (first 15 LinkedIn DMs): Link to landing page
- Group B (next 15 LinkedIn DMs): Link directly to sample report page

**Metrics:**
- Click-through rate (did they click the link?)
- Conversion rate (did they subscribe?)
- Time on page (Analytics)

**Decision criteria:**
- If Group B converts ≥2x better, use sample report link as default
- If Group A converts better, landing page copy is working

**Timeline:** Results available by Monday 3/23

---

### Experiment 2: AI Narrative vs. Raw Data (Content Format)
**Hypothesis:** GovCon BD professionals prefer AI-generated insights over raw data tables.

**Setup:**
- Week 3: Send 10 subscribers the current AI narrative format
- Week 3: Send 10 subscribers a raw data table (awards + NAICS + value + links only)
- Week 4: Survey both groups: "Which format do you prefer? Why?"

**Metrics:**
- Open rate
- Click-through rate
- Reply rate
- Survey responses

**Decision criteria:**
- If raw data group has ≥2x engagement, pivot to data-first format
- If >60% say they prefer raw data, deprioritize AI narrative

**Timeline:** Results available by Week 4 (need 20 subscribers first)

---

### Experiment 3: Weekly vs. Daily Cadence (Future)
**Hypothesis:** Weekly is optimal, but need to test with real users.

**Setup:** (Deferred until 50+ subscribers)
- Survey: "Would you prefer daily, weekly, or monthly?"
- Track open rates over 4 weeks—do they decline (suggesting too frequent)?

**Not testing this in Week 2—focus on acquisition first.**

## Kill Criteria

### Immediate Kill Signals (Stop and Pivot)
1. **<3 subscribers by Friday 3/21** → Outreach model is broken. Emergency diagnosis:
   - Is the value prop clear?
   - Are we targeting the wrong people?
   - Is the market too small?
   - Do people not understand what we're offering?

2. **0% conversion rate on warm contacts** → If personal network won't subscribe, strangers definitely won't. Pivot to services (consulting) instead of newsletter.

3. **>50% of replies are "I already have this"** → We're solving a solved problem. Incumbents (GovWin, Deltek) already own this space.

### Week 2 Checkpoint Criteria (Sunday 3/22)
**If we hit <5 subscribers by end of Sunday:**
- Pause outreach
- Conduct 5 phone calls with non-subscribers: "I reached out about the newsletter. You didn't sign up. Why not?"
- Document objections
- Decide: Pivot message, pivot audience, or pivot model

### Week-End Kill Criteria (Wednesday 3/25)
**If we hit <10 subscribers by end of Week 2:**
- The TAM is too small OR the value prop is wrong
- Emergency pivot session: Review assumption audit (`/Users/luke/Personal/govcon-intel/strategy/assumptions.md`)
- Options:
  - **Pivot 1:** Narrow to 1-2 verticals instead of 9 (be #1 in cyber instead of #4 in everything)
  - **Pivot 2:** Change format (daily digest, Slack community, real-time alerts instead of weekly newsletter)
  - **Pivot 3:** Change business model (fractional BD consulting instead of newsletter)
  - **Pivot 4:** Shut down and return to day job

**If we hit 10-24 subscribers:**
- On track but below target
- Diagnosis: Is conversion rate the problem (need better messaging) or reach the problem (need more outreach)?
- Decision: Double down on what's working. If warm emails converted at 50% and LinkedIn DMs at 5%, do more warm intros (ask for referrals).

**If we hit 25+ subscribers:**
- Celebrate for 5 minutes, then get back to work
- Week 3 priority: Scale the channel that worked best
- If LinkedIn DMs worked: Send 50 more
- If warm emails worked: Ask early subscribers for 3 referrals each
- If organic worked: Double down on content (daily posts instead of 3/week)
